# Desserts and Sweet Dishes

## Baklava
Cuisine: Turkish/Greek/Levantine | Technique: layering + baking + soaking | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 24 pieces | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 45 min

Ingredients: 500g phyllo pastry (thawed), 300g unsalted butter (clarified or melted), 400g mixed nuts (walnuts, pistachios — roughly chopped), 2 tsp cinnamon, 1/2 tsp cardamom Syrup: 300g sugar, 200ml water, 150g honey, 1 tsp rose water (or orange blossom water), 1 cinnamon stick, 2 cardamom pods, strip of lemon zest

Instructions:
1. Syrup (make first — must be cold when poured over hot baklava): Combine sugar, water, cinnamon, cardamom, and lemon zest. Bring to boil, stir to dissolve.
2. Syrup (make first — must be cold when poured over hot baklava): Simmer 10 minutes until slightly syrupy. Remove from heat, add honey and rose water.
3. Syrup (make first — must be cold when poured over hot baklava): Cool completely. Refrigerate.
4. Baklava: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Butter a 30×40cm baking dish.
5. Baklava: Combine chopped nuts with cinnamon and cardamom.
6. Baklava: Layer 8 sheets of phyllo in the dish, brushing EACH sheet generously with clarified butter before adding the next. Work quickly — phyllo dries out fast. Keep unused phyllo covered.
7. Baklava: Spread 1/3 of the nut mixture evenly.
8. Baklava: Layer 4 more buttered phyllo sheets. Add another 1/3 of nuts.
9. Baklava: Layer 4 more buttered phyllo sheets. Add final 1/3 of nuts.
10. Baklava: Top with remaining phyllo sheets (8+), buttering each generously, including the top.
11. Baklava: Using a very sharp knife, cut all the way through to the bottom in a diamond pattern. This step MUST be done before baking.
12. Baklava: Bake 40–45 minutes until golden and flaky.

## Chocolate Fondant (Lava Cake)
Cuisine: French/British | Technique: baking precisely timed | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 6 | Prep: 20 min + chilling | Cook: 12 min

Ingredients: 200g dark chocolate (70%), 200g unsalted butter, 4 whole eggs, 4 egg yolks, 200g sugar, 80g all-purpose flour, cocoa powder for dusting, butter for greasing

Instructions:
1. Butter 6 ramekins (180ml) generously. Dust with cocoa powder, tapping out excess.
2. Melt chocolate and butter together in a double boiler or microwave (30-second bursts). Stir until smooth. Let cool slightly.
3. Whisk whole eggs, yolks, and sugar together until pale and slightly thickened.
4. Pour chocolate mixture into eggs. Mix gently.
5. Sift in flour. Fold until just combined — do not overmix.
6. Divide batter among ramekins. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes (can be refrigerated up to 24 hrs).
7. Bake 10–12 minutes — the edges should be set but center should still jiggle. Time this precisely — 1 minute makes the difference between molten and set.
8. Rest 1 minute in the ramekin. Run a knife around the edge. Invert onto a plate.
9. Serve with vanilla ice cream or fresh cream. Eat immediately. The magic: the outer cake sets; the interior remains liquid chocolate. CRÈME CARAMEL (Classic Flan)

## Crème Brûlée
Cuisine: French | Technique: bain-marie baking + torching | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 6 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 45 min + chilling

Ingredients: 600ml heavy cream, 1 vanilla bean (split and scraped), 6 egg yolks, 100g sugar, + 6 tsp superfine sugar for the brûlée crust

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
2. Warm cream with vanilla bean and seeds in a saucepan until just below simmering. Remove from heat. Let steep 15 minutes.
3. Whisk egg yolks and sugar together until pale and slightly thickened.
4. Temper eggs: slowly pour warm cream into yolks in a thin stream while whisking constantly. Do this gradually — if cream is too hot and added too fast, eggs scramble.
5. Strain through a fine mesh. Skim any bubbles from the surface.
6. Pour into 6 shallow ramekins. Place in a deep roasting pan.
7. Fill the pan with boiling water to come halfway up the ramekins.
8. Bake 30–35 minutes until the custard is just set at the edges but still jiggles slightly in the center when shaken. Do not overbake — it will continue to set as it cools.
9. Remove from water bath. Cool to room temperature. Refrigerate at least 4 hours (overnight best).
10. When ready to serve: sprinkle 1 tsp superfine sugar evenly over each custard.
11. Using a kitchen torch, caramelize the sugar: hold torch 5cm away, move in slow circles. Sugar melts → bubbles → turns amber → becomes a brittle glassy crust.
12. Wait 2 minutes for the caramel to fully harden. Serve immediately.

## Panna Cotta
Cuisine: Italian | Technique: gelification (cold set) | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 6 | Prep: 15 min + 4 hrs setting | Cook: 5 min

Ingredients: 600ml heavy cream, 100ml whole milk, 80g sugar, 1 vanilla bean (split) or 2 tsp vanilla paste, 3 gelatin sheets (or 1.5 tsp powdered gelatin dissolved in 2 tbsp cold water) Serve with: fresh berry coulis, strawberries, or balsamic-macerated berries

Instructions:
1. Soak gelatin sheets in cold water until soft, about 5 minutes.
2. Combine cream, milk, sugar, and vanilla in a saucepan. Heat until steaming and sugar dissolves. Do NOT boil.
3. Remove from heat. Squeeze excess water from gelatin sheets. Add to hot cream. Stir to dissolve.
4. Strain through a fine mesh. Remove vanilla bean.
5. Pour into lightly oiled ramekins or glasses.
6. Refrigerate at least 4 hours until set. Overnight is better.
7. To unmold: run a thin knife around the edge. Place a plate on top. Flip confidently. Or serve directly in glasses without unmolding (easier, looks beautiful too).
8. Top with fresh berries or berry coulis. The texture: should tremble and just barely hold its shape — not rubbery, not liquid. ## Tarte Tatin
Cuisine: French (Sologne) | Technique: caramelizing + inverting | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 6–8 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 40 min

Ingredients: 6–8 firm apples (Golden Delicious, Granny Smith, or Braeburn — not soft varieties), 150g sugar, 80g unsalted butter, 200g puff pastry (or shortcrust), juice of 1/2 lemon, pinch of salt, vanilla ice cream to serve

Instructions:
1. Peel, core, and quarter apples. Toss with lemon juice.
2. In a 24–26cm oven-safe skillet or tarte tatin pan, melt butter over medium heat.
3. Add sugar in an even layer. Cook without stirring until sugar begins to melt and caramelize around the edges, then stir gently to caramelize evenly.
4. Continue cooking until caramel is deep amber (170°C on a candy thermometer). Remove immediately — it continues cooking.
5. Arrange apple quarters very tightly in the caramel, standing upright in concentric circles. They will shrink significantly in the oven — pack them in.
6. Return to medium heat. Cook apples in caramel 10–15 minutes until they soften slightly and caramel bubbles up around them.
7. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
8. Roll pastry slightly larger than the pan. Drape over the apples, tucking edges down around the apples (like pulling a blanket down around the edges).
9. Bake 20–25 minutes until pastry is deep golden.
10. Rest 5 minutes (critical — too hot and caramel runs everywhere).
11. Invert decisively onto a plate. If apples stick, gently reposition.
12. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream. The caramel should be thick and glossy. CHOCOLATE FONDANT (Molten Lava Cake)

## Tiramisu
Cuisine: Italian (Veneto) | Technique: assembling + setting overnight | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 8–10 | Prep: 30 min + overnight chilling | Cook: 0

Ingredients: 6 egg yolks, 120g sugar, 500g mascarpone (room temperature), 300ml strong espresso (cooled), 3 tbsp Marsala wine or dark rum, 250ml heavy cream, 200g Savoiardi (ladyfingers), cocoa powder for dusting

Instructions:
1. Combine egg yolks and sugar in a heatproof bowl set over simmering water (double boiler).
2. Whisk vigorously for 8–10 minutes until pale, tripled in volume, and reaches 160°F (71°C). This step is both the cooking of the eggs (food safety) and the creation of the zabaglione.
3. Remove from heat. Beat with electric mixer on high until completely cooled and thick.
4. Add mascarpone to the cooled zabaglione. Fold gently until smooth.
5. In a separate bowl, whip heavy cream to soft peaks. Fold into mascarpone mixture in two additions. Be gentle — you want to keep the airiness.
6. Combine espresso and Marsala in a shallow bowl.
7. Quickly dip ladyfingers — 1–2 seconds only, not soaking through completely. The interior should stay slightly dry; the outside absorbs coffee. Over-soaked = mushy.
8. Arrange a single layer of dipped ladyfingers in a 30×20cm dish (or individual glasses).
9. Spread half the mascarpone cream over the ladyfingers.
10. Add second layer of dipped ladyfingers. Spread remaining cream on top.
11. Cover and refrigerate overnight (minimum 6 hours — flavors meld dramatically).
12. Dust generously with fine cocoa powder immediately before serving.

Ingredients: 4 egg whites, meringue shell, 1 cup whipped cream, berries, 2 cups flour, sugar, butter, 4 eggs, 1 tbsp oil

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven as appropriate for the dish (typically 325–375°F / 160–190°C).
2. Prepare pan/ramekins: grease, line, or water-bath setup as needed.
3. Mix wet ingredients, then combine with dry ingredients just until smooth (do not overmix).
4. Pour/shape into pan. Bake until set: edges set and center slightly springy or a tester comes out clean (as appropriate).
5. Cool as directed (often 10–30 minutes), then chill or serve warm depending on the dessert.
6. Serve with recommended topping (fruit, sauce, whipped cream, etc.).
