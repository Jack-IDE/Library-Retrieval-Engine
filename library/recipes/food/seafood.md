# Seafood Main Dishes

## Fish Tacos
Cuisine: Baja Mexican | Technique: deep-frying or grilling | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 600g firm white fish (halibut, cod, or mahi-mahi), 12 small corn tortillas Beer batter: 1 cup flour, 1 cup cold beer, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp chili powder, salt Cabbage slaw: 2 cups green cabbage (shredded), 1 tbsp lime juice, 1 tbsp mayo, salt, pepper Chipotle crema: 3 tbsp sour cream, 1 tbsp chipotle in adobo (minced), 1 tsp lime juice Garnish: lime wedges, pickled jalapeños, fresh cilantro, fresh salsa

Instructions:
1. Make slaw: toss cabbage with lime juice, mayo, salt, and pepper. Refrigerate.
2. Make crema: combine sour cream, chipotle, and lime juice. Set aside.
3. Make batter: whisk flour and spices together. Stir in cold beer until smooth. The batter should coat the back of a spoon. Do not overmix — lumps are fine.
4. Heat oil to 350°F (175°C) in a wide, deep pot.
5. Cut fish into 2-inch strips. Season with salt. Pat dry.
6. Dip fish into batter, letting excess drip off.
7. Fry in batches, not overcrowding, 3–4 minutes until golden and crispy. Drain on rack.
8. Warm tortillas directly over a gas flame 30 seconds per side (or in a dry pan).
9. Assemble: slaw on tortilla, beer-battered fish on top, chipotle crema drizzle, cilantro, jalapeño. Squeeze lime over. Eat immediately.

## Moules Marinières
Cuisine: French (Belgian origin) | Technique: steaming in wine | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 2 kg fresh mussels, 3 shallots (finely minced), 3 cloves garlic (minced), 200ml dry white wine (something you'd drink), 50g unsalted butter, 3 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped), cream (optional — 100ml for a richer version), salt, black pepper, crusty baguette

Instructions:
1. Clean mussels: scrub shells with a stiff brush under cold running water. Pull out the "beard" (the stringy bit protruding from the shell) with a sharp tug. Discard any mussels with broken shells or those that are open and do not close when firmly tapped — they are dead and unsafe.
2. Melt half the butter in a large, deep pot with a tight-fitting lid over medium heat.
3. Add shallots. Cook until softened, 3 minutes.
4. Add garlic. Cook 1 minute.
5. Pour in white wine. Bring to a boil.
6. Add ALL the mussels at once. Cover tightly. Increase heat to high.
7. Cook 3–5 minutes, shaking the pot once or twice, until all mussels have opened.
8. Discard any mussels that remain firmly closed after 5 minutes.
9. Remove from heat. Add remaining butter and cream (if using). Add parsley.
10. Taste the broth — it is pure umami. Season with pepper (rarely needs salt).
11. Serve immediately in large bowls with the broth poured over. Essential: a side of frites or baguette to soak up the broth.

## Seared Scallops with Pea Purée
Cuisine: Modern British-French | Technique: very high-heat searing | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 12 large sea scallops (dry-packed, not wet-packed), salt, white pepper, 2 tbsp neutral oil Pea purée: 400g frozen peas (thawed), 50g butter, 4 tbsp crème fraîche or cream, salt, mint To garnish: 6 rashers bacon (cooked crisp and crumbled), fresh mint, microgreens

Instructions:
1. Pea purée: Bring salted water to boil. Add peas. Cook 2 minutes. Drain.
2. Pea purée: Blend peas with butter, crème fraîche, salt, and a few mint leaves until very smooth.
3. Pea purée: Pass through a fine sieve for silky consistency. Season. Keep warm.
4. Scallops: Remove the tough muscle (small, slightly firm piece on the side). Pat scallops COMPLETELY dry — moisture = no sear. Season both flat faces with salt only (pepper burns).
5. Scallops: Heat a cast iron or stainless steel pan over the highest heat until smoking.
6. Scallops: Add oil (smoke point must be high). Add scallops in a single layer — not touching.
7. Scallops: DO NOT MOVE. Sear 1.5–2 minutes until a deep golden-brown crust forms on the bottom.
8. Scallops: Flip once. Cook 1 minute maximum on the second side. Perfectly cooked scallop: golden sear, center still slightly translucent.
9. Scallops: Remove immediately. Rest 30 seconds.
10. Plate: Spread a smear of pea purée on each plate. Place 3 scallops on each.
11. Plate: Scatter crispy bacon, microgreens, and a few mint leaves. Squeeze lemon over. PANNA COTTA (Vanilla)

## Shrimp Scampi
Cuisine: Italian-American | Technique: sautéing | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 600g large shrimp (peeled, deveined, tails on), 400g linguine, 6 cloves garlic (thinly sliced), 150ml dry white wine, 100ml chicken or seafood stock, 60g unsalted butter, 3 tbsp EVOO, 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes, juice of 1 lemon, 3 tbsp fresh parsley, salt, pepper

Instructions:
1. Cook linguine in well-salted boiling water until 1 minute before al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water. Drain.
2. Pat shrimp completely dry. Season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over high heat until shimmering.
4. Add shrimp in a single layer. Sear 1 minute without moving until pink on the bottom. Flip. Cook 30 seconds more — just until opaque. Remove immediately. They will finish cooking in the sauce. Overcooked shrimp are rubbery — remove early.
5. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter to the pan. When foaming, add garlic and chili flakes. Cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant and just starting to color.
6. Add white wine. Increase heat. Reduce by half.
7. Add stock. Reduce by half again.
8. Add drained pasta. Toss vigorously, adding pasta water to create a glossy sauce.
9. Return shrimp to the pan. Toss to coat.
10. Add lemon juice and parsley. Toss. Serve immediately. FISH TACOS (Baja Style)

## Sole Meunière
Cuisine: French classic | Technique: dredging + pan-frying in beurre noisette | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 2 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 2 whole Dover sole (or 4 large sole fillets), 100g unsalted butter, 4 tbsp flour, 2 tbsp neutral oil, 2 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped), 2 tbsp lemon juice, flaky salt, pepper

Instructions:
1. Pat sole completely dry. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Dredge in flour, coating lightly on both sides. Shake off all excess flour.
3. Heat oil and 30g butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat until butter foams.
4. Add fish presentation-side down. Cook 3–4 minutes until golden. Do not move.
5. Carefully flip. Cook 2–3 more minutes on the second side.
6. Remove fish to warm plates.
7. In the same pan, add remaining butter. Cook over medium heat until butter turns golden brown and smells nutty — beurre noisette. Watch carefully; it goes from browned to burned in seconds.
8. Add lemon juice (it will spatter). Swirl. Add parsley.
9. Pour beurre noisette immediately over the fish. Serve with steamed asparagus and boiled new potatoes. WHOLE STEAMED FISH (Cantonese)

## Whole Steamed Fish (Cantonese)
Cuisine: Cantonese | Technique: steaming | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 2–4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 1 whole sea bass, trout, or red snapper (600–800g, gutted and scaled), 5cm ginger (julienned), 3 scallions (julienned — keep green and white parts separate), 2 tbsp Shaoxing wine, 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tsp sugar, 3 tbsp neutral oil, 1 tbsp sesame oil, white pepper, salt

Instructions:
1. Score fish diagonally on both sides, 3 cuts per side, to the bone.
2. Season fish inside cavity and all over with salt and white pepper.
3. Stuff cavity with half the ginger and white parts of scallion.
4. Set up steamer: bring water to boil in a wok. Place fish on a heatproof plate.
5. Combine soy sauce, wine, and sugar. Pour over fish.
6. Steam fish 8–10 minutes per 500g of fish weight. Fish is done when flesh flakes easily at the thickest part. Overcooking is the most common error — check early.
7. Carefully drain any liquid from the plate. Lay remaining ginger and scallion greens over the fish.
8. Heat oil and sesame oil in a small pan until smoking hot.
9. Pour sizzling oil directly over the ginger and scallion — they will crackle dramatically, perfuming the fish. This is the flavor-finishing moment.
10. Serve immediately at the table, fish on the plate.

## Black Pepper Crab
