# Rice and Grain Dishes

## Chicken Biryani (Hyderabadi — dum pukht)
Cuisine: Hyderabadi | Technique: dum (sealed vessel steaming) | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Hard | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4–6 | Prep: 3 hrs (including marinating) | Cook: 1 hr

Ingredients: Chicken marinade: 1kg chicken pieces, 300g yogurt, 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 2 tsp Kashmiri chili powder, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp garam masala, 2 tbsp fried onions (birista), 1 tbsp ghee, juice of 1 lemon, salt, handful mint and cilantro Rice: 600g basmati (soaked 30 min), water for parboiling, 2 bay leaves, 4 cloves, 4 cardamom, 1 cinnamon stick, 1 tsp cumin, salt, 1 tbsp ghee Birista (fried onions): 4 onions (thinly sliced) deep-fried in oil until dark golden brown Dum assembly: 1/2 cup warm milk infused with 1/4 tsp saffron, extra ghee

Instructions:
1. Birista (make first): Fry thinly sliced onions in hot oil until dark brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels. They will crisp more as they cool.
2. Marinate chicken: Combine all marinade ingredients with 3/4 of the birista. Coat chicken thoroughly. Marinate at least 2 hours (overnight preferred). Room temperature for last 30 min.
3. Parboil rice: Bring a large pot of water to boil. Add whole spices, ghee, and generous salt.
4. Parboil rice: Add soaked and drained rice. Cook until 70% done (it will still have a white center when you bite it). This is very important — overcooked rice = mushy biryani.
5. Parboil rice: Drain immediately.
6. Dum assembly: Layer marinated chicken in a heavy-bottomed pot (Dutch oven ideal). Arrange evenly.
7. Dum assembly: Add half the parboiled rice over chicken. Scatter some birista, mint, and cilantro.
8. Dum assembly: Add remaining rice. Scatter remaining birista and herbs on top.
9. Dum assembly: Drizzle saffron milk over the top rice layer. Drizzle ghee generously.
10. Dum assembly: Seal the pot tightly — use foil under the lid if needed to trap steam completely.
11. Dum assembly: Cook on medium-high heat 5 minutes (to build steam), then reduce to the very lowest heat possible for 25 minutes. Alternatively, place pot on a tawa (flat griddle) to diffuse heat evenly.
12. Dum assembly: Turn off heat. Rest 10 minutes without lifting the lid.

## Kimchi Fried Rice
Cuisine: Korean | Technique: stir-frying | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Very Easy | Serves: 2 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 3 cups day-old cooked rice (cold, not fresh), 1 cup well-fermented kimchi (chopped), 100g pork belly or bacon (sliced), 2 eggs, 1 tbsp gochujang, 2 tbsp kimchi juice, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, 2 scallions (sliced), sesame seeds, neutral oil

Instructions:
1. Cook pork belly or bacon in a wok or large skillet over high heat until lightly crisped.
2. Add kimchi. Stir-fry 2 minutes until slightly caramelized.
3. Add gochujang. Stir-fry 30 seconds.
4. Add cold rice. Break up any clumps. Press rice against pan to let it sear — don't stir constantly. Let it sit 1 minute to develop a slight crust.
5. Add kimchi juice and soy sauce. Toss everything together.
6. Push rice to one side. Fry eggs sunny-side-up in the open space.
7. Drizzle sesame oil over the rice. Toss everything once more.
8. Plate rice, slide fried egg on top (yolk unbroken). Top with scallions and sesame seeds.
9. Break the egg yolk tableside — it becomes the sauce.

## Oyakodon
Cuisine: Japanese (donburi) | Technique: simmering + egg drop on rice | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 2 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 2 chicken thighs (boneless, skinless — cut in 2cm pieces), 4 eggs, 1/2 onion (thinly sliced), 2 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tbsp mirin, 1 tbsp sake, 200ml dashi, 1 tsp sugar, 2 bowls cooked Japanese short-grain rice, mitsuba or scallions to garnish

Instructions:
1. Combine dashi, soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar in a small bowl — this is the tsuyu.
2. In a small, shallow skillet (traditional oyakodon pan or small nonstick pan), bring tsuyu to a simmer over medium heat.
3. Add sliced onion. Cook until just softened, 3 minutes.
4. Add chicken pieces in a single layer. Cook on one side 3 minutes without moving.
5. Flip chicken. Cook another 2 minutes.
6. Beat 2 eggs lightly — leaving some streaks of white visible (this creates texture).
7. Pour eggs in a circular motion over the chicken. Cover with a lid.
8. Cook 1–2 minutes until white is just set but yolk is still slightly runny. (The Japanese preference is hanjuku — half-cooked egg. Pull it off early.)
9. Slide entire contents over a bowl of steamed rice. The loose egg should settle into the rice beautifully.
10. Garnish with mitsuba or scallions. Make one serving at a time for best results.

## Paella Valenciana
Cuisine: Valencian | Technique: wide pan, no stirring, socarrat crust | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Hard | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 40 min

Ingredients: 400g Bomba or Calasparra rice, 1 chicken (cut into 8 pieces), 500g rabbit (cut in pieces), 200g flat green beans (Bajoca), 200g butter beans (garrofón — or lima beans), 400g ripe tomatoes (grated), 1 tsp sweet paprika, 1/4 tsp saffron (steeped in hot water), 4 tbsp olive oil, 1.2L chicken stock, rosemary sprig, salt Note: No onion, no garlic, no stock cubes — authentic Valencian paella is austere.

Instructions:
1. Heat olive oil in a 40cm paella pan over medium-high heat (or use two burners on stove).
2. Season chicken and rabbit generously. Brown all pieces skin-side down until golden, about 5 minutes per side. The caramelization is flavor — do not rush.
3. Push meat to the edges. Add green beans and butter beans to the center. Sauté until beans show a little color, 3–4 minutes.
4. Add grated tomato. Cook stirring until darkened and paste-like, about 5 minutes.
5. Add paprika. Stir into tomato and cook 30 seconds — do not burn (goes bitter fast).
6. Immediately add all the hot stock and saffron water. Bring to a vigorous boil. Season with salt — more than you think. The stock should be slightly over-seasoned because the rice will absorb salt.
7. Add rice in a cross pattern, spreading evenly across the pan. Do not stir again from this point forward. This is essential — stirring releases starch and prevents socarrat.
8. Lay rosemary on top. Cook on high heat 10 minutes.
9. Reduce to medium. Cook 8 more minutes.
10. Reduce to low. Place rosemary sprig directly on coals/gas to smoke slightly.
11. In the last 2 minutes, increase heat to high — this creates the socarrat (the prized crust at the bottom). Listen for a gentle crackling sound.
12. Remove from heat. Cover with newspaper or clean towels for 5 minutes to rest.

## Poke Bowl
Cuisine: Hawaiian | Technique: assembling | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 2 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 20 min (rice)

Ingredients: 300g sushi-grade ahi tuna (cut in 2cm cubes), 2 cups cooked short-grain sushi rice, 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp sesame oil, 1 tsp sriracha, 1 tsp mirin, 1/2 cup edamame (frozen, cooked), 1/2 cucumber (sliced), 1 avocado (sliced), 2 radishes (sliced thin), nori strips, sesame seeds, pickled ginger, tobiko (optional)

Instructions:
1. Season rice with sushi vinegar while warm. Fan to cool (or cool at room temperature).
2. Toss tuna gently with soy sauce, sesame oil, sriracha, and mirin. Marinate 10 minutes.
3. Arrange rice in two bowls.
4. Arrange tuna, edamame, cucumber, avocado, and radish in sections over the rice — each ingredient in its own "wedge" for visual appeal.
5. Drizzle any remaining tuna marinade over the bowl.
6. Top with nori strips, sesame seeds, pickled ginger, and tobiko.
7. Serve immediately. Mix before eating. BEEF RENDANG (Indonesian/Malaysian)

## Polenta (basic)
Cuisine: Northern Italian | Technique: long stirring | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy (just time) | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 45 min

Ingredients: 250g coarse cornmeal (polenta), 1.2L water, 1.2L whole milk (or all water for simpler version), 80g unsalted butter, 100g Parmigiano Reggiano (grated), 1.5 tsp fine salt

Instructions:
1. Bring water and milk to a boil in a large heavy-bottomed pot.
2. Add salt. Reduce heat to medium.
3. Slowly pour in polenta in a thin, steady stream, whisking constantly to prevent lumps.
4. Continue whisking for the first 5 minutes until fully incorporated.
5. Reduce heat to very low. Cook, stirring frequently with a wooden spoon, for 40–45 minutes. It is done when it pulls cleanly away from the sides of the pot.
6. Remove from heat. Beat in cold butter vigorously until melted.
7. Add Parmesan. Beat until smooth and glossy.
8. Season and serve immediately — polenta sets quickly. Softer serving: add more hot water or milk for a looser, pourable consistency (like mashed potato). Set and fried: pour onto an oiled tray, cool, refrigerate 2 hours, then slice and pan-fry in butter. MASHED POTATO (Robuchon Style)

## Risotto ai Funghi Porcini
Cuisine: Italian | Technique: stirring method | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 30 min

Ingredients: 320g Arborio or Carnaroli rice, 30g dried porcini mushrooms, 300g fresh cremini or mixed mushrooms, 1.2L vegetable or light chicken stock, 1 shallot (minced), 2 cloves garlic (minced), 150ml dry white wine, 60g unsalted butter (cold, cubed), 60g Parmigiano Reggiano (finely grated), 3 tbsp EVOO, small bunch fresh thyme or parsley, salt, black pepper

Instructions:
1. Soak dried porcini in 300ml hot water for 20 minutes. Lift them out carefully — do not pour (sand settles at the bottom). Finely chop soaked porcini. Strain the soaking liquid through a coffee filter or fine cloth — add to your stock.
2. Bring combined stock + porcini liquid to a gentle simmer in a separate pot. Keep at a low simmer throughout cooking.
3. Clean and slice fresh mushrooms. Sauté in 1 tbsp butter over high heat until golden. Season. Set aside.
4. In a large, wide pan (ideally wide and shallow), heat olive oil over medium heat. Add shallot. Cook until softened, 5 minutes.
5. Add rice. Toast the rice, stirring constantly, for 2 minutes until edges turn translucent. You'll hear a faint clicking sound — that's right.
6. Add white wine. Stir constantly until completely absorbed.
7. Add chopped porcini. Add one ladleful of simmering stock. Stir gently but constantly until absorbed. Add another ladle. Continue in this rhythm — never adding too much liquid at once, never letting it dry out completely.
8. After 16–18 minutes, the rice should be al dente — cooked but with a slight bite. Remove from heat (crucial — residual heat will keep cooking).
9. Mantecatura (the most important step): Add cold butter cubes and Parmesan all at once. Vigorously beat and fold the butter into the risotto off the heat for 2 minutes. The risotto should become glossy, creamy, and flow like lava when the pan is tipped.
10. Stir in sautéed fresh mushrooms. Season. Rest 1 minute with lid on.
11. Serve immediately in warm bowls. Risotto waits for no guest — guests wait for risotto.

## Mansaf
Cuisine: Jordanian | Technique: long simmering + layering | Time: 45 min | Diet: gluten-free-ish

Ingredients: 500g lamb, 1 cup mansaf rice, jameed broth, flatbread, fried nuts, 1 onion, 2 cloves garlic, 1 lemon, 2 cups stock or water

Instructions:
1. Season protein (if used) with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a heavy pot.
2. Brown protein on all sides; remove to a plate.
3. Sauté aromatics (onion/garlic) until soft. Add spices and toast briefly.
4. Add main ingredients and enough liquid (stock/tomato/coconut milk) to partially cover.
5. Bring to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook until tender, stirring occasionally.
6. Adjust seasoning. Finish with herbs/acid as appropriate and serve.

## Nasi Goreng
Cuisine: Indonesian | Technique: stir-frying | Time: 30 min | Diet: gluten-free-ish

Ingredients: 4 egg, 500g chicken, 500g shrimp, 1 cup Indonesian fried rice, kecap manis, sambal, fried shallot, 1 onion, 2 cloves garlic, 1 lemon

Instructions:
1. Prep: slice proteins thin and cut vegetables evenly. Mix sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
2. Heat a wok/skillet over high heat. Add a thin film of oil.
3. Cook protein first until just browned. Remove to a plate.
4. Add aromatics (garlic/ginger/onion). Stir 15–30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add vegetables from firm to tender. Stir-fry until crisp-tender.
6. Return protein to the pan. Pour in sauce and toss until glossy and cooked through.
7. Finish with herbs/sesame/lime (if used). Serve hot with rice/noodles.
