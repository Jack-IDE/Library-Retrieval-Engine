# Supplemental Techniques and Notes

## CHURROS with Chocolate Dipping Sauce
Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 6 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 250ml water, 1 tbsp sugar, 1/2 tsp salt, 2 tbsp unsalted butter, 150g all-purpose flour, 2 eggs, oil for deep frying, cinnamon sugar (100g sugar + 1 tbsp cinnamon) Chocolate sauce: 200g dark chocolate, 200ml heavy cream, 1 tbsp honey

Instructions:
1. Bring water, sugar, salt, and butter to a boil.
2. Remove from heat. Add all flour at once. Beat vigorously with a wooden spoon until dough comes together and pulls from the sides. Return to medium heat, stir 1 minute to dry out the dough slightly.
3. Transfer to a bowl. Beat in eggs one at a time — the dough will initially look curdled, then come back together smooth and glossy.
4. Fill a piping bag with a large star tip (1–2cm). Heat oil to 375°F (190°C).
5. Pipe dough directly into hot oil, cutting with scissors at 15–20cm lengths.
6. Fry in batches, turning, until deep golden and crispy all around — about 3 minutes.
7. Drain on paper towels. Roll immediately in cinnamon sugar.
8. Chocolate sauce: Heat cream until steaming. Pour over chopped chocolate. Wait 1 minute. Stir from center outward until smooth. Add honey.
9. Serve churros warm with chocolate sauce for dipping.

## CRÈME CARAMEL (Classic Flan)
Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 6 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 1 hr + chilling

Ingredients: Caramel: 150g sugar, 3 tbsp water Custard: 500ml whole milk, 200ml heavy cream, 4 whole eggs, 2 egg yolks, 100g sugar, 1 tsp vanilla

Instructions:
1. Caramel: Combine sugar and water in a small saucepan. Cook over medium-high heat — DO NOT STIR. Swirl the pan occasionally if needed. Cook until deep amber (about 170°C).
2. Caramel: Quickly pour caramel into the bottoms of 6 ramekins. Swirl to coat bottoms. Set aside. The caramel will set solid — this is correct.
3. Custard: Warm milk and cream to steaming. Do not boil.
4. Custard: Whisk eggs, yolks, and sugar until combined. Slowly pour in warm milk while whisking. Add vanilla. Strain through a fine mesh.
5. Custard: Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C).
6. Custard: Pour custard over the set caramel in the ramekins. Place in a roasting pan.
7. Custard: Add boiling water to the pan to come halfway up the ramekins.
8. Custard: Bake 40–50 minutes until just set — a slight jiggle in the center is correct.
9. Custard: Remove from water bath. Cool. Refrigerate minimum 4 hours (overnight is better).
10. Custard: To unmold: run a thin knife around the edge. Cover with a flat plate. Invert with a confident, decisive flip. The caramel sauce will flow down over the custard.

## FRENCH MACARONS
Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Very Hard | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: makes ~24 sandwiched macarons | Prep: 1 hr | Cook: 15 min + 24 hrs rest

Ingredients: 150g almond flour, 150g powdered sugar (sifted), 55g aged egg whites (left uncovered in fridge 3 days) Italian meringue: 150g sugar, 50ml water, 55g aged egg whites, pinch of cream of tartar Food coloring (gel, not liquid) Filling options: ganache (200g chocolate + 100ml cream), buttercream, jam

Instructions:
1. Sift almond flour and powdered sugar together twice. Weigh exactly.
2. Add 55g egg whites directly to the almond-sugar mixture. Do not mix yet.
3. Italian meringue: cook sugar and water to 244°F (118°C) — "soft ball" stage.
4. Meanwhile, beat the other 55g egg whites to soft peaks.
5. Pour hot sugar syrup in a thin stream into whipping whites, avoiding the whisk. Beat on high until meringue is stiff, glossy, and cooled to room temperature.
6. Add food coloring to meringue if using.
7. Macaronage: Add 1/3 of meringue to the almond mixture. Fold aggressively to loosen. Add remaining meringue. Fold in a specific motion: cut down the center, sweep around the sides, fold over top. Repeat 40–60 times. The batter should flow like lava and fall from the spatula in a thick ribbon that disappears back into the batter within 10 seconds. Under-mixed = rough tops; over-mixed = flat pancakes.
8. Pipe in 4cm rounds onto parchment-lined trays. Drop tray firmly on counter 3–4 times to release air bubbles.
9. Rest at room temperature 45–60 minutes until a dry skin forms — the tops should not stick to a finger when gently touched.
10. Bake at 300°F (150°C) for 14–15 minutes. The "feet" (the ruffled skirt) should form.
11. Cool completely before removing from parchment.
12. Fill and sandwich. Refrigerate 24 hours before serving — the rest melds flavors and creates the perfect chewy texture. Patience is mandatory.

## FRESH SPRING ROLLS (Gỏi Cuốn — Vietnamese)
Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 4 (makes ~12 rolls) | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 12 rice paper wrappers, 200g cooked large shrimp (halved lengthwise), 200g pork belly (boiled and thinly sliced), 100g rice vermicelli (cooked), 1 head butter lettuce, fresh mint, fresh cilantro, fresh Thai basil, 1 carrot and 1/2 cucumber (julienned), Peanut dipping sauce: 3 tbsp hoisin, 2 tbsp peanut butter, 1 tbsp soy, 1 tsp lime juice, 1 garlic clove (minced), warm water to thin, crushed peanuts, sriracha

Instructions:
1. Peanut Sauce: Combine hoisin, peanut butter, soy, lime, and garlic. Thin with warm water to a dipping consistency. Top with crushed peanuts and drizzle of sriracha.
2. Rolling: Fill a wide, shallow dish with warm water.
3. Rolling: Dip one rice paper wrapper in warm water for 10–15 seconds until just pliable — it will continue to soften as you work. Do not over-soak.
4. Rolling: Lay flat on a damp cutting board or clean kitchen towel.
5. Rolling: Place a lettuce leaf in the lower third of the wrapper.
6. Rolling: Add a small bundle of vermicelli, carrot, cucumber, a few herb leaves, and 2 slices pork.
7. Rolling: Begin rolling from the bottom, folding sides in after one turn.
8. Rolling: Before the final fold, place 3 shrimp halves (pink-side down) so they will be visible through the wrapper. Complete the roll, pressing to seal.
9. Rolling: Serve immediately or cover with a damp towel for up to 1 hour.

## GROUNDNUT SOUP / MAAFE (West African Peanut Stew)
Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4–6 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 1 hr

Ingredients: 1 kg chicken pieces or beef (cubed), 200g smooth peanut butter, 400g canned tomatoes, 1 large onion (diced), 4 cloves garlic (minced), 1 tbsp fresh ginger (grated), 2 tbsp tomato paste, 2 cups chicken stock, 1 Scotch bonnet or habanero pepper (whole), 1 sweet potato (cubed), 2 cups spinach or greens, 2 tbsp groundnut oil or vegetable oil, salt

Instructions:
1. Season meat generously with salt. Brown in hot oil in batches. Remove.
2. In the same pot, cook onion until softened, 5 minutes. Add garlic and ginger, 1 minute.
3. Add tomato paste. Cook 2 minutes until darkened.
4. Add canned tomatoes, stock, and browned meat. Bring to a boil.
5. Reduce heat and add the whole Scotch bonnet (leave whole — it flavors without too much heat; pierce for medium heat; mince for very hot).
6. Add sweet potato. Simmer 20 minutes.
7. In a bowl, whisk peanut butter with a ladle of hot cooking liquid until smooth. Stir this peanut butter mixture back into the pot. This prevents it from seizing.
8. Simmer 20 more minutes, stirring occasionally, until stew is thick and rich.
9. Add greens in last 5 minutes. Season with salt.
10. Remove whole Scotch bonnet. Serve over white rice, fufu, or with crusty bread.

## PESTO GENOVESE with Trofie
Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 400g trofie or linguine, 2 medium potatoes (peeled, cubed small), 100g green beans Pesto: 2 large bunches fresh basil (about 50g leaves only — no stems), 30g pine nuts, 2 cloves garlic, 60g Parmesan (grated), 30g Pecorino (grated), 100ml EVOO, salt, ice

Instructions:
1. Pesto (mortar method — traditional): Toast pine nuts in a dry pan until lightly golden. Cool completely.
2. Pesto (mortar method — traditional): Pound garlic with a pinch of coarse salt in a large mortar until a paste forms.
3. Pesto (mortar method — traditional): Add pine nuts. Pound until broken down but not completely smooth.
4. Pesto (mortar method — traditional): Add basil leaves in batches. Pound with a circular motion, not stabbing — you're grinding, not pulverizing. The basil will release brilliant green oil.
5. Pesto (mortar method — traditional): Add cheeses. Incorporate with the pestle.
6. Pesto (mortar method — traditional): Stir in olive oil to achieve a loose, spreadable consistency. Season with salt. 1b. Blend garlic, pine nuts, salt, and basil. Add cheese. Blend briefly. With machine running, pour in olive oil. Do not over-process — pesto should have texture. Tip: Chill basil in ice water before blending to preserve green color.
7. Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to boil.
8. Pasta: Add potato cubes. Cook 5 minutes. Add green beans. Cook 3 more minutes.
9. Pasta: Add pasta. Cook until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water.
10. Pasta: Drain everything together (pasta, potatoes, and beans cook together — a Ligurian tradition).
11. Pasta: Toss with pesto, adding pasta water to loosen if needed. Serve immediately. SPAGHETTI ALLE VONGOLE (with Clams)

## SOURDOUGH COUNTRY LOAF
Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Hard | Diet: vegan | Serves: 12 | Prep: 1 day (feeding) + 8 hrs active | Cook: 45 min

Ingredients: 450g bread flour, 50g whole wheat flour, 375ml cold water, 100g active sourdough starter (fed 4–12 hours ago, at peak activity), 9g fine sea salt

Instructions:
1. Morning (or 8 hours before baking): Autolyse: Mix flour and 350ml water until no dry flour remains. Cover and rest 30–60 min.
2. Morning (or 8 hours before baking): Add starter: Pour starter over dough. Mix by squeezing through fingers until combined. Rest 30 minutes.
3. Morning (or 8 hours before baking): Add salt: Dissolve salt in remaining 25ml water. Drizzle over dough. Incorporate fully.
4. Morning (or 8 hours before baking): Bulk fermentation: Over the next 4 hours at room temperature (around 75°F/24°C): Perform 4 sets of stretch-and-folds, one every 30 minutes. For each set, stretch the dough from four sides and fold over itself (north, south, east, west = one set). Dough will become stronger, smoother, and develop air bubbles.
5. Morning (or 8 hours before baking): After bulk fermentation, the dough should have grown 50–75% and feel airy and alive.
6. Shaping: Gently turn dough onto an unfloured surface. Pre-shape into a rough round. Rest 20 min.
7. Shaping: Final shape: Flip dough. Fold the top third over the middle, then the sides, then roll toward you, building surface tension. Transfer seam-side up into a floured proofing basket (banneton) or floured bowl lined with a floured towel.
8. Proofing: Cover and refrigerate overnight (8–16 hours) — cold retard develops flavor and makes scoring easier.
9. Baking: Preheat oven to 500°F (260°C) with a Dutch oven inside for 1 hour.
10. Baking: Turn cold dough onto parchment. Score decisively with a lame or razor — one long slash at 45° angle, about 1cm deep.
11. Baking: Lower into screaming-hot Dutch oven. Cover. Bake 20 minutes (steam trapped = crust forms).
12. Baking: Remove lid. Reduce to 450°F (232°C). Bake 20–25 more minutes until deep mahogany.

## SOUTHERN FRIED CHICKEN
Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Medium | Serves: 4 | Prep: 8+ hrs brining | Cook: 30 min

Ingredients: 1 whole chicken (cut in 8 pieces) or 8 bone-in pieces Brine: 1L buttermilk, 2 tbsp hot sauce, 1 tbsp salt, 1 tbsp sugar Flour dredge: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 cup cornstarch, 2 tsp garlic powder, 2 tsp onion powder, 2 tsp smoked paprika, 2 tsp salt, 1 tsp cayenne, 1 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp thyme Oil: 1L vegetable or peanut oil for frying

Instructions:
1. Brine: Combine buttermilk, hot sauce, salt, and sugar. Add chicken. Cover and refrigerate minimum 4 hours — overnight best.
2. Combine all flour dredge ingredients in a wide shallow bowl.
3. Remove chicken from brine. Do not rinse — the buttermilk coating helps dredge adhere.
4. Dredge each piece in seasoned flour, pressing firmly to adhere. Shake off excess.
5. Optional double dredge for extra crust: dip back in buttermilk, then dredge again in flour.
6. Let coated chicken rest on a wire rack for 15 minutes — coating will adhere better.
7. Heat oil to 325°F (165°C) in a large Dutch oven or cast iron pot.
8. Fry dark meat first (thighs, drumsticks): 15–18 minutes, turning occasionally. Breast pieces: 12–14 minutes. Keep oil between 300–325°F.
9. Chicken is done at internal temperature 165°F (74°C) and coating is deep golden.
10. Drain on a wire rack — NOT paper towels (rack keeps the bottom crispy).
11. Season immediately with flaky salt. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

## TABBOULEH (Lebanese)
Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan | Serves: 4 | Prep: 30 min + 30 min rest | Cook: 0

Ingredients: 3 large bunches flat-leaf parsley (finely chopped — this is the main ingredient), 1 small bunch fresh mint (finely chopped), 1/2 cup fine bulgur wheat, 3 medium ripe tomatoes (very finely diced), 4 scallions (finely sliced), juice of 2 lemons, 4 tbsp EVOO, salt, black pepper

Instructions:
1. Rinse bulgur wheat. Soak in cold water 20 minutes. Drain through a fine mesh. Squeeze out excess water. Fluff with a fork.
2. Wash and dry parsley thoroughly (wet herbs = soggy salad). Remove as many stems as possible. Very finely chop — tabbouleh should be almost minced, not roughly chopped.
3. Finely chop mint leaves.
4. Combine bulgur, parsley, mint, tomato, and scallions in a large bowl.
5. Dress with lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
6. Toss well. Taste — it should be bright, sharply lemony, and very herbaceous.
7. Let rest 30 minutes before serving to allow flavors to meld.
8. Serve with romaine lettuce leaves for scooping, or with pita. Note: In Lebanon, tabbouleh is an herb salad with a little bulgur — NOT a bulgur salad with a little parsley. The ratio matters: 80% herbs, 20% bulgur maximum. SOM TUM (Green Papaya Salad)
