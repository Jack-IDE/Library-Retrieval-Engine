# Pasta and Noodle Dishes

## Cacio e Pepe
Cuisine: Italian (Roman) | Technique: pasta water emulsion | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Hard (deceptively technical) | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 400g tonnarelli or thick spaghetti, 200g Pecorino Romano (finely grated, room temp), 50g Parmigiano Reggiano (finely grated), 2 tsp black pepper (coarsely toasted and cracked), salt

Instructions:
1. Toast black pepper in a dry large skillet until fragrant. Crack coarsely.
2. Combine cheeses in a bowl. Mix together.
3. Cook pasta in less water than usual (1.5L instead of 4L) — more concentrated starch is your emulsifier. Salt lightly — the cheese is salty.
4. While pasta cooks, add a ladleful of pasta water to the skillet with the pepper. Bring to a very gentle simmer.
5. Drain pasta 2 minutes before al dente. Reserve 2 cups pasta water.
6. Transfer pasta to the pepper skillet over low heat. Add a splash of water. Toss.
7. Move the pan off the heat. Add cheese gradually in three additions, tossing constantly and adding tiny splashes of pasta water between each addition.
8. The cheese must melt smoothly into the starchy water — never clump. If clumping: more pasta water + more tossing + off the heat.
9. The sauce should coat the pasta like a creamy glaze, not pool at the bottom.
10. Serve immediately with extra Pecorino and cracked pepper. Note: Perfect cacio e pepe is one of the hardest things to cook well. The ratio, temperature, and pasta water starch are everything. PESTO GENOVESE with Trofie

## Dan Dan Mian (Dan Dan Noodles)
Cuisine: Sichuan | Technique: assembling | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Medium | Serves: 2 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: Noodles: 200g Chinese wheat noodles (or spaghetti) Pork topping: 200g ground pork, 2 tbsp ya cai (Yibin preserved mustard — optional), 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine, 1 tsp sugar Sauce (per bowl): 2 tbsp Chinese sesame paste (or tahini), 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp Chinkiang black vinegar, 1 tsp Sichuan pepper (ground), 2 tbsp chili oil (homemade preferred), 1 tsp sugar, 2 cloves garlic (minced), 1 tbsp sesame oil, 3–4 tbsp hot pasta water to loosen Toppings: blanched bok choy, sesame seeds, scallions, extra chili oil

Instructions:
1. Cook pork: Heat oil in a wok. Add pork and stir-fry, breaking up clumps, until completely browned. Add ya cai (if using), soy sauce, wine, and sugar. Cook until fragrant and slightly sticky. Set aside.
2. Sauce: In each serving bowl, combine sesame paste, soy, vinegar, Sichuan pepper, chili oil, sugar, garlic, and sesame oil. Mix well.
3. Cook noodles in boiling water until just tender. Reserve 1/2 cup noodle water.
4. Add hot noodle water to each bowl — about 4 tbsp — to loosen the sauce. Mix well.
5. Blanch bok choy briefly in the noodle water.
6. Drain noodles. Add to bowls. The noodles go into the sauce, not the other way around.
7. Top with ground pork mixture, bok choy, scallions, sesame seeds, and a generous drizzle of chili oil.
8. Mix thoroughly before eating — the pleasure is in the tossing. TAGLIATELLE AL RAGÙ BOLOGNESE (The Real One)

## Pad Thai
Cuisine: Thai | Technique: stir-frying | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Medium | Serves: 2–4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 200g dried rice noodles (sen lek — 3mm wide), 200g shrimp or chicken or tofu, 2 eggs, 100g firm tofu (cubed, optional), 150g bean sprouts, 3 scallions (cut in 2cm pieces) Sauce: 3 tbsp fish sauce, 3 tbsp tamarind concentrate (or 2 tbsp lime juice + 1 tbsp sugar), 1 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar, 1 tsp oyster sauce Aromatics: 3 tbsp dried shrimp, 3 tbsp shallots (sliced), 2 tbsp preserved radish (chai poh — optional), 3 tbsp vegetable oil To serve: bean sprouts, lime wedges, crushed roasted peanuts, dried chili flakes, sugar, fish sauce

Instructions:
1. Soak noodles in cold water 30 minutes until pliable but not fully cooked.
2. Mix together sauce ingredients (tamarind, fish sauce, sugar, oyster sauce). Taste — should be sweet, sour, and salty in balance.
3. Heat wok over highest heat until smoking. Add oil.
4. Add protein (shrimp/chicken) — cook until just done. Push to side or remove.
5. Add shallots and dried shrimp. Stir-fry 30 seconds.
6. Add drained noodles. Stir-fry 1–2 minutes.
7. Pour sauce over noodles. Toss and cook until absorbed.
8. Push noodles to one side. Crack eggs into the wok. Scramble briefly.
9. Before fully set, fold noodles over the egg. Toss everything together.
10. Add bean sprouts and scallions (and tofu if using). Toss 30 seconds — keep sprouts crunchy.
11. Serve with the full condiment set: extra bean sprouts, lime, peanuts, dried chili, sugar, and fish sauce so guests can adjust to their taste. PASTA ALLA NORMA (Sicilian Eggplant Pasta)

## Pasta alla Norma
Cuisine: Sicilian | Technique: frying + simmering | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 30 min

Ingredients: 400g rigatoni or spaghetti, 2 large eggplants (cubed 2cm), 400g crushed tomatoes, 4 cloves garlic (sliced), 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes, 100ml olive oil for frying eggplant, 3 tbsp EVOO for sauce, handful fresh basil, 100g ricotta salata (or firm ricotta), salt

Instructions:
1. Salt eggplant cubes generously and leave in a colander for 20 minutes. Rinse and pat completely dry. (Salting removes bitterness and prevents oil absorption.)
2. Heat 100ml olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Fry eggplant in batches until golden and tender, about 5–6 minutes per batch. Do not crowd. Drain on paper towels.
3. In a separate wide pan, heat EVOO over medium. Add garlic and chili flakes. Cook 1 minute until golden.
4. Add crushed tomatoes. Season with salt. Simmer 15 minutes until slightly thickened.
5. Cook pasta in well-salted boiling water to al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water.
6. Add fried eggplant to tomato sauce. Toss gently.
7. Drain pasta. Combine with sauce, adding pasta water to loosen if needed.
8. Plate and top with torn basil and generous grating of ricotta salata. DAN DAN MIAN (Sichuan Dan Dan Noodles)

## Spaghetti Carbonara
Cuisine: Italian (Roman) | Technique: tempering eggs + pasta water emulsion | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Medium (technique-sensitive) | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 400g spaghetti or rigatoni, 200g guanciale (cured pork cheek — or pancetta if unavailable), 4 large egg yolks + 1 whole egg, 100g Pecorino Romano (finely grated), 30g Parmigiano Reggiano (finely grated), 2 tsp freshly cracked black pepper (toasted and coarsely ground), salt

Instructions:
1. Bring a large pot of water to boil. Salt generously (it should taste like the sea).
2. Cut guanciale into thick matchsticks (lardons). In a cold large pan, add guanciale. Cook over medium heat, starting cold, until fat renders and guanciale is crispy and golden, about 8 minutes. Remove from heat. Do not discard the rendered fat.
3. In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, whole egg, Pecorino, and Parmesan until smooth. Season with cracked black pepper. The mixture should be thick.
4. Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until 2 minutes BEFORE al dente (it will finish in the pan). Reserve at least 2 full cups of pasta cooking water before draining.
5. Transfer drained pasta directly to the pan with guanciale (which should be off heat or on very low heat). Add a ladleful of hot pasta water. Toss vigorously.
6. Let the pasta cool for 30 seconds (critical — if too hot, eggs will scramble).
7. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta. Toss constantly and vigorously, adding pasta water a small splash at a time to create a glossy, creamy sauce.
8. The key: the residual heat of the pasta cooks the eggs gently. Toss continuously — if scrambled bits appear, too hot. Add cold water splash and toss immediately.
9. Plate quickly. Top with extra Pecorino and another generous grind of black pepper. Absolute rules: No cream. No onion. No garlic. No olive oil. No shortcuts.

## Spaghetti alle Vongole
Cuisine: Italian (Naples) | Technique: steaming clams | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 400g spaghetti or linguine, 1.5 kg fresh littleneck or Manila clams, 4 cloves garlic (thinly sliced), 2 dried red chilis (crumbled) or 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes, 150ml dry white wine, 4 tbsp EVOO, 3 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley (chopped), salt

Instructions:
1. Purge clams: Soak in cold salted water (30g salt per liter) for 30 minutes. Scrub shells under cold running water. Discard any that are already open and don't close when tapped — they are dead.
2. Cook pasta in heavily salted boiling water until 2 minutes before al dente.
3. Meanwhile: heat olive oil in a large, wide pan over high heat. Add garlic and chili. Cook until golden and fragrant, about 1 minute — watch carefully, garlic burns instantly.
4. Add clams. Pour in white wine. Cover immediately with a tight lid.
5. Shake the pan and cook on high heat until all clams open, about 3–5 minutes. Discard any clams that refuse to open.
6. Using a slotted spoon, remove most clams from the pan. Shell about half; leave the rest in shells for presentation.
7. Drain pasta 2 minutes early, reserving 1 cup pasta water. Add pasta directly to the clam pan with the cooking liquid.
8. Toss over medium heat, adding splashes of pasta water until pasta is al dente and sauce coats the pasta glossily.
9. Return clams to the pan. Add parsley. Toss to combine.
10. Serve immediately — no cheese on seafood pasta (Italian rule).

## Tagliatelle al Ragù Bolognese
Cuisine: Bologna | Technique: long simmering (3+ hrs) | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 6 | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 3–4 hrs

Ingredients: 600g fresh tagliatelle or 400g dried, 150g beef (minced), 150g pork (minced), 100g veal (minced — or extra pork), 150g pancetta (minced), 1 onion (finely minced), 1 carrot (minced), 1 celery stalk (minced), 200ml whole milk, 200ml dry white wine, 200ml beef broth, 2 tbsp tomato paste, 150g whole canned tomatoes (just a little), 3 tbsp butter, 3 tbsp EVOO, salt, pepper, Parmigiano to serve Note: No garlic. No herbs. No cream at the end. No shortcuts.

Instructions:
1. Mince the soffritto (onion, carrot, celery) as finely as possible — brunoise.
2. Melt butter with olive oil in a large heavy pot over medium heat.
3. Cook soffritto with a generous pinch of salt for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until deeply softened and lightly golden.
4. Add pancetta. Cook until rendered, about 5 minutes.
5. Increase heat to high. Add all meats. Cook, breaking up clumps with a wooden spoon, until all liquid has evaporated and meat is lightly browned — this takes 15 minutes. Don't rush it — proper browning is essential flavor development.
6. Add white wine. Stir and cook until completely evaporated.
7. Add milk. Stir and cook until completely absorbed. The milk tenderizes the meat.
8. Add tomato paste. Stir and cook 2 minutes.
9. Add canned tomatoes (crushed by hand). Add broth. Bring to a gentle simmer.
10. Reduce heat to the gentlest possible simmer — barely a bubble. Cook uncovered 3 hours, adding broth splashes if it dries. The ragù should be rich, tender, and barely saucy — not a tomato sauce with meat in it.
11. Cook pasta in abundant salted water until al dente. Reserve pasta water.
12. Add a ladleful of pasta water to the ragù. Add drained pasta. Toss to coat.

## Yaki Udon
Cuisine: Japanese | Technique: stir-frying | Time: 30 min

Ingredients: 500g pork or shrimp, udon, soy, mirin, cabbage, mushrooms, 1 onion, 2 cloves garlic, 1 lemon, soy sauce

Instructions:
1. Prep: slice proteins thin and cut vegetables evenly. Mix sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
2. Heat a wok/skillet over high heat. Add a thin film of oil.
3. Cook protein first until just browned. Remove to a plate.
4. Add aromatics (garlic/ginger/onion). Stir 15–30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add vegetables from firm to tender. Stir-fry until crisp-tender.
6. Return protein to the pan. Pour in sauce and toss until glossy and cooked through.
7. Finish with herbs/sesame/lime (if used). Serve hot with rice/noodles.

## Zhajiangmian
Cuisine: Beijing | Technique: simmering sauce | Time: 45 min

Ingredients: 500g pork, 1 cup thick wheat noodles, fermented soybean paste (zha jiang), cucumber, soy, 1 onion, 2 cloves garlic, 1 lemon, 2 cups stock or water

Instructions:
1. Season protein (if used) with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a heavy pot.
2. Brown protein on all sides; remove to a plate.
3. Sauté aromatics (onion/garlic) until soft. Add spices and toast briefly.
4. Add main ingredients and enough liquid (stock/tomato/coconut milk) to partially cover.
5. Bring to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook until tender, stirring occasionally.
6. Adjust seasoning. Finish with herbs/acid as appropriate and serve.
