# Appetizers and Small Plates

## Baba Ghanoush
Cuisine: Middle Eastern | Technique: charring eggplant + blending | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 40 min

Ingredients: 3 large eggplants, 3 tbsp tahini, 2 cloves garlic (minced), 2 tbsp lemon juice, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, 2 tbsp EVOO, salt, fresh parsley, pomegranate seeds (optional)

Instructions:
1. Char the eggplants: Place whole eggplants directly over a gas burner flame (or under a broiler as close as possible). Cook, turning every 4–5 minutes, until the skin is completely charred and the eggplant is totally collapsed — about 20 minutes. It should feel like a deflated balloon. This charring IS the flavor.
2. Transfer to a colander or bowl. Let cool 10 minutes until handleable.
3. Peel away all charred skin. Don't rinse — you'll wash away the smoky flavor. A few bits of char are fine and add depth.
4. Place eggplant flesh in a colander and let drain for 10 minutes — excess water = watery baba ghanoush.
5. Chop or mash eggplant with a fork — keep it slightly textured, not completely smooth.
6. Mix in tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and salt. Taste and adjust.
7. Spread on a plate. Drizzle with EVOO. Dust with smoked paprika.
8. Scatter parsley and pomegranate seeds. Serve with pita or vegetables.

## Bruschetta al Pomodoro
Cuisine: Italian | Technique: grilling + no-cook topping | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Very Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 5 min

Ingredients: 4–8 thick slices sourdough or ciabatta, 400g very ripe tomatoes (assorted), 1 large garlic clove, 8–10 fresh basil leaves, 4 tbsp EVOO, flaky sea salt, black pepper, 1 tbsp red wine vinegar

Instructions:
1. Dice tomatoes into 1cm pieces. Combine in a bowl with basil (torn), EVOO, vinegar, salt, and pepper. Let sit at room temperature 10 minutes.
2. Grill or broil bread slices until golden and crisp with char marks on both sides.
3. While hot, rub one side of each slice vigorously with the cut garlic clove. The rough texture of the toasted bread will grate the garlic — less is more.
4. Drizzle rubbed bread with more EVOO.
5. Top with tomato mixture at the last moment. Serve immediately — bruschetta waits for no one. Soggy bruschetta is a failure.

## Falafel
Cuisine: Middle Eastern | Technique: deep-frying | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 6 (makes ~24 falafel) | Prep: 10 min + overnight soak | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 500g dried chickpeas (NOT canned — this is non-negotiable), 1 large onion (roughly chopped), 1 cup fresh parsley, 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, 4 cloves garlic, 1 tbsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp baking powder, 1.5 tsp salt, oil for deep frying Serve with: pita, tahini, Israeli salad, pickled vegetables

Instructions:
1. Soak dried chickpeas overnight in plenty of cold water (they will triple in size). Do NOT use canned — they are too wet and the falafel will fall apart.
2. Drain chickpeas thoroughly.
3. In a food processor: pulse chickpeas until they resemble coarse sand — not smooth, not chunky. Some whole pieces are fine. This texture is critical.
4. Add onion, parsley, cilantro, garlic, cumin, coriander, and salt. Pulse until combined but still slightly coarse — never smooth.
5. Transfer to a bowl. Mix in baking powder.
6. Refrigerate mixture 30 minutes (makes shaping easier and improves texture).
7. Test: fry one small ball. If it falls apart, refrigerate longer or add 1–2 tbsp flour.
8. Form into balls or patties using wet hands or a falafel scoop.
9. Heat oil to 350°F (175°C). Fry in batches of 6–8 for 3–4 minutes until deep golden brown.
10. Drain on paper towels. Serve immediately in pita with tahini, salad, pickles.

## Guacamole
Cuisine: Mexican | Technique: mashing | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Very Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 0

Ingredients: 3 ripe Hass avocados, 1 lime (juiced), 1/2 white onion (finely diced), 1 jalapeño (seeded for mild; seeds included for hot — finely minced), 2 tbsp fresh cilantro leaves (chopped), 1/2 tsp fine salt, 1 small tomato (seeded and diced — optional)

Instructions:
1. Halve avocados, remove stones. Scoop flesh into a bowl.
2. Add lime juice and salt. Mash with a fork — leave chunky (not smooth like a paste).
3. Fold in onion, jalapeño, cilantro, and tomato.
4. Taste. A great guacamole needs more salt and lime than you think.
5. Serve immediately with tortilla chips. To store: press plastic wrap directly against the surface (prevents oxidation). Will keep 1–2 hours. Tip: The avocado stone in the bowl does NOT prevent browning — that's a myth. Only lime juice and minimizing air contact slow oxidation.

## Gyoza (pan-fried)
Cuisine: Japanese | Technique: pan-frying + steaming | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Serves: 4 (makes ~30 dumplings) | Prep: 45 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: Filling: 300g ground pork, 150g napa cabbage (very finely chopped), 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp sesame oil, 1 tsp sake or dry sherry, 1 tbsp ginger (grated), 2 cloves garlic (minced), 2 scallions (minced), 1 tsp sugar, white pepper 30 round dumpling wrappers (gyoza wrappers), 2 tbsp neutral oil, 100ml water Dipping sauce: 3 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp rice vinegar, chili oil

Instructions:
1. Finely mince cabbage. Toss with 1/2 tsp salt and let sit 10 minutes. Squeeze out ALL moisture with your hands. This is critical — wet cabbage = soggy gyoza.
2. Combine pork, squeezed cabbage, soy, sesame oil, sake, ginger, garlic, scallion, sugar, and white pepper. Mix vigorously with a fork or your hand until sticky and well-combined. The mixture should feel cohesive, not loose.
3. Place one wrapper on your palm. Add 1 generous tsp filling to the center.
4. Moisten the rim of the wrapper with water. Fold the wrapper over the filling to create a half-moon. Pleat the top edge: make 5–6 small folds, pressing each fold firmly against the back unpleated edge to seal. Place flat-side down.
5. Heat oil in a large nonstick or cast iron skillet over medium-high heat.
6. Place gyoza flat-side down in a single layer. Cook until golden underneath, about 2 min.
7. Add 100ml water (it will spit — step back). Cover immediately with a lid.
8. Steam 5–6 minutes until water has evaporated. Remove lid. Let cook another 1–2 minutes until bottoms are again crispy and golden.
9. Serve flat-side up on a plate. Serve with dipping sauce. FRESH SPRING ROLLS (Gỏi Cuốn — Vietnamese)

## Hummus
Cuisine: Lebanese/Middle Eastern | Technique: blending | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 6–8 | Prep: 15 min (+ overnight soak if using dried) | Cook: 1 hr

Ingredients: 400g dried chickpeas (or 2 × 400g cans — dried is significantly better), 1 tsp baking soda, 150g good-quality tahini, juice of 2 lemons, 3 cloves garlic (minced to paste), 1 tsp salt, ice water (2–4 tbsp), EVOO, paprika, cumin, fresh parsley to serve

Instructions:
1. From dried chickpeas (best method): Soak chickpeas overnight in cold water with 1 tsp baking soda. They will double in size.
2. From dried chickpeas (best method): Drain, rinse well. Cook in a large pot with fresh water and 1/2 tsp baking soda. Boil until completely tender and skins are falling off, about 45–60 minutes. Skim off floating skins — the more you remove, the smoother the hummus.
3. From dried chickpeas (best method): Reserve 1 cup cooking liquid. Drain chickpeas.
4. Blending: While chickpeas are still hot (this is key for smooth hummus), add to food processor.
5. Blending: Process 3 minutes until a smooth paste forms.
6. Blending: Add tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and salt. Process 3 more minutes.
7. Blending: With machine running, drizzle in ice water 1 tbsp at a time until hummus is silky, creamy, and slightly lighter in color. The ice water whitens and aerates it.
8. Blending: Taste: should be nutty, lemony, garlicky, and deeply savory. Adjust.
9. Blending: Spread in a bowl with the back of a spoon, creating a well in the center.
10. Blending: Fill the well with EVOO. Dust with paprika and cumin. Top with parsley. Serve with warm pita.

## Shrimp Scampi (starter portion)
Cuisine: Italian-American | Technique: sautéing | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 600g large shrimp (peeled, deveined, tails on), 400g linguine, 6 cloves garlic (thinly sliced), 150ml dry white wine, 100ml chicken or seafood stock, 60g unsalted butter, 3 tbsp EVOO, 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes, juice of 1 lemon, 3 tbsp fresh parsley, salt, pepper

Instructions:
1. Cook linguine in well-salted boiling water until 1 minute before al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water. Drain.
2. Pat shrimp completely dry. Season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over high heat until shimmering.
4. Add shrimp in a single layer. Sear 1 minute without moving until pink on the bottom. Flip. Cook 30 seconds more — just until opaque. Remove immediately. They will finish cooking in the sauce. Overcooked shrimp are rubbery — remove early.
5. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter to the pan. When foaming, add garlic and chili flakes. Cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant and just starting to color.
6. Add white wine. Increase heat. Reduce by half.
7. Add stock. Reduce by half again.
8. Add drained pasta. Toss vigorously, adding pasta water to create a glossy sauce.
9. Return shrimp to the pan. Toss to coat.
10. Add lemon juice and parsley. Toss. Serve immediately. FISH TACOS (Baja Style)

## Steak Tartare
Cuisine: French | Technique: fine knife work, no-cook | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Medium (precision + food safety) | Serves: 2 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 0

Ingredients: 300g beef tenderloin (center cut — top quality, very fresh), 1 tbsp capers (finely chopped), 1 shallot (finely minced), 1 tbsp Dijon mustard, 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce, 2 tsp cognac or brandy (optional), 2 egg yolks (finest quality, pasteurized preferred), 4 cornichons (finely chopped), 1 tsp hot sauce (Tabasco), 1 tbsp fresh parsley (minced), salt, pepper, toasted baguette or frites to serve

Instructions:
1. Keep beef very cold throughout — work quickly. Using a sharp knife, trim all silver skin and fat.
2. Slice beef into 3mm slices. Stack and cut into 3mm strips. Cut across into 3mm dice. All by hand — never use a food processor (ruins texture).
3. In a chilled bowl, gently combine beef, capers, shallot, Dijon, Worcestershire, cognac, cornichons, hot sauce, and parsley.
4. Season with salt and black pepper. Mix gently with a fork.
5. Taste — it should be bright, peppery, sharp with mustard, and deeply savory.
6. Form into a neat mound on a chilled plate using a ring mold if desired.
7. Create a small indent in the top. Place one egg yolk in the indent.
8. Serve immediately with toast points, frites, or in lettuce cups. Safety note: Use only the freshest possible beef from a trusted butcher. Freeze briefly (20 min) before cutting can reduce bacterial risk. GYOZA (Pan-Fried Japanese Dumplings)
