# Meat Main Dishes

## Beef Bourguignon
Cuisine: French | Technique: long braising | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Serves: 6 | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 3–4 hrs

Ingredients: 1.5 kg beef chuck (cut in 5cm cubes), 750ml Burgundy or Pinot Noir, 200g lardons or thick-cut bacon, 300g pearl onions (or 2 medium onions, quartered), 400g cremini mushrooms (quartered), 2 carrots (roughly chopped), 2 celery stalks, 1 onion (quartered), 4 garlic cloves, 3 tbsp tomato paste, 2 tbsp flour, 500ml beef broth, bouquet garni (bay, thyme, parsley), 3 tbsp butter, 2 tbsp neutral oil, salt, pepper, fresh parsley

Instructions:
1. Optional overnight marinade: combine beef with wine, carrots, celery, onion, garlic, and bouquet garni. Marinate overnight in fridge for deeper flavor.
2. Remove beef from marinade (if used). Pat completely dry — crucial for browning. Strain and reserve wine. Discard marinated vegetables.
3. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
4. In a large Dutch oven over high heat, heat oil until smoking. Brown beef in batches, not touching each other — 3 minutes per side. Each batch should be deeply browned. Remove and set aside. Do not rush this.
5. Reduce heat. Add lardons. Cook until fat renders, 5 minutes.
6. Add onions and carrots. Cook until lightly browned, 5 minutes.
7. Add garlic and tomato paste. Cook 2 minutes.
8. Sprinkle flour over everything. Stir and cook 2 minutes.
9. Pour in reserved wine and broth. Bring to a boil, scraping up all browned bits.
10. Return beef to pot. Add bouquet garni. Liquid should come 3/4 up the beef.
11. Cover and transfer to oven. Cook 2.5–3 hours until beef is completely tender and a fork slides in with no resistance.
12. Meanwhile, sauté pearl onions in butter until golden (15 min). Separately, sauté mushrooms in butter over high heat until golden (5 min). Set both aside.

## Beef Rendang
Cuisine: Indonesian/Malaysian | Technique: long simmering until dry | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 6 | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 3 hrs

Ingredients: 1 kg beef chuck (cut in 4cm cubes), 400ml coconut milk, 3 stalks lemongrass (bruised), 4 kaffir lime leaves, 1 turmeric leaf (optional), 1 cinnamon stick Spice paste (rempah): 6 shallots, 4 garlic cloves, 5cm galangal, 3cm ginger, 6 dried red chilis (soaked in hot water), 3 candlenuts or macadamia nuts, 1 tsp turmeric

Instructions:
1. Blend all rempah ingredients to a smooth paste.
2. Heat a large wok or pot over medium heat. Fry rempah paste in 3 tbsp oil, stirring constantly, until fragrant and darkened — about 10 minutes. The paste should look crumbly and separated from oil.
3. Add beef. Coat thoroughly in the paste. Cook 5 minutes.
4. Add coconut milk, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and cinnamon stick.
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer. Cook uncovered.
6. Stir occasionally. As the coconut milk reduces, stir more frequently to prevent sticking.
7. After 2–2.5 hours, the liquid will have mostly evaporated. Now the rendang will begin to fry in its own residual coconut oil. At this stage, stir constantly.
8. Continue until the beef is dark brown, almost dry, and caramelized. This drying stage is what makes rendang unique — it's deliberately cooked "dry."
9. Taste for salt. Remove lemongrass and cinnamon. Serve with rice and sambal.

## Beef Wellington
Cuisine: British/French | Technique: searing + wrapping + baking | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Hard | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4–6 | Prep: 1 hr | Cook: 40 min + resting

Ingredients: 800g–1kg beef tenderloin (center-cut, trimmed, room temperature), 500g mixed mushrooms (finely chopped — duxelles), 100g prosciutto or Parma ham (thin slices), 2 tbsp Dijon mustard, 500g puff pastry, 2 egg yolks (beaten for egg wash), 2 tbsp butter, 2 shallots (minced), 2 cloves garlic (minced), 1 tbsp fresh thyme, 100ml dry sherry or white wine, salt, pepper

Instructions:
1. Duxelles: Finely dice all mushrooms (pulse in food processor to a rough paste).
2. Duxelles: Melt butter over high heat. Add shallots and garlic. Cook 3 minutes.
3. Duxelles: Add mushrooms and thyme. Cook over high heat, stirring constantly, until all moisture has completely evaporated — 15–20 minutes. The duxelles should be completely dry. This is critical — moisture = soggy pastry. Add sherry, cook until absorbed. Season well. Cool completely.
4. Beef: Season beef generously with salt and pepper.
5. Beef: Sear in screaming-hot oil on all sides including the ends — 1 minute per surface. The beef must be very well-browned. Remove. Brush immediately with Dijon mustard all over.
6. Beef: Cool completely (refrigerate 20 minutes if pressed for time).
7. Assembly: Lay a large sheet of plastic wrap on the counter. Arrange prosciutto slices in a slightly overlapping rectangle large enough to wrap the beef completely.
8. Assembly: Spread duxelles evenly over the prosciutto, leaving a 2cm border.
9. Assembly: Place beef at the near edge. Roll tightly in the plastic wrap, twisting ends like a candy.
10. Assembly: Refrigerate 30 minutes minimum — the roll must hold its shape before wrapping in pastry.
11. Assembly: Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
12. Assembly: Roll puff pastry into a rectangle large enough to wrap the beef. Remove plastic from beef roll.

## Carnitas
Cuisine: Mexican | Technique: slow confit in lard | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 8–10 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 3–4 hrs

Ingredients: 2 kg pork shoulder (bone-in preferred), 1 orange (halved), 1 lime (halved), 1 head garlic (halved crosswise), 2 tsp cumin, 2 tsp oregano (dried), 2 tsp salt, 1 tsp pepper, 1 tsp chili powder, 1 bay leaf, 500ml lard or neutral oil (enough to partially submerge) Serve with: corn tortillas, diced onion, cilantro, salsa, lime

Instructions:
1. Cut pork into 8–10 large chunks. Pat dry.
2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, combine pork, halved orange and lime (squeeze juice in first, then add the halves), garlic, cumin, oregano, chili powder, salt, pepper, and bay leaf.
3. Add enough lard to come halfway up the pork.
4. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
5. Reduce heat to medium-low. Cook uncovered, turning pork every 30 minutes, for 2.5–3 hours. The liquid will gradually evaporate. This is correct — the pork will eventually fry in the rendered fat as the water cooks off.
6. In the last 30 minutes, the pork will be sizzling and beginning to brown. Turn pieces more frequently. The outside should be golden and slightly crispy.
7. Remove pork. Shred with two forks, discarding large fat pieces.
8. For extra crispiness (popular in taquerias): spread on a baking sheet and broil 3–5 min.
9. Serve in warm corn tortillas with diced white onion, fresh cilantro, salsa, and lime.

## Cassoulet
Cuisine: Languedocien | Technique: very long braising + baking | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 6–8 | Prep: 1 day (soaking) + 30 min | Cook: 3 hrs

Ingredients: 500g dried white cannellini or Tarbais beans (soaked overnight), 4 duck confit legs, 400g Toulouse sausages (or pork sausages), 200g pancetta or pork belly, 1 onion (diced), 4 garlic cloves, 400g canned tomatoes, 500ml chicken stock, bouquet garni, breadcrumbs, duck fat, salt, pepper

Instructions:
1. Cook soaked beans in fresh water 45 minutes until just tender but not falling apart. Drain.
2. Brown sausages in duck fat. Remove. Brown pancetta. Remove.
3. In the same pot, cook onion and garlic until soft. Add tomatoes. Cook 10 minutes.
4. Add beans, sausages, pancetta, stock, and bouquet garni. Season.
5. Simmer 30 minutes until beans are very tender and liquid has reduced to a thick sauce.
6. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
7. Transfer to a deep casserole dish. Nestle duck confit legs on top, skin-side up.
8. Scatter a thick layer of breadcrumbs over the top.
9. Bake 1 hour. The crust will form and crack — tradition dictates breaking the crust back into the stew and baking again (some say 7 times). A cassoulet is never done until the crust has been broken and reformed 2–3 times.
10. The final crust should be golden and the beans rich and unctuous.

## Chicken Tikka Masala
Cuisine: British-Indian (disputed) | Technique: grilling/broiling + simmering | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min + 2 hrs marinating | Cook: 40 min

Ingredients: Chicken tikka: 600g chicken thighs (boneless), 150g yogurt, 2 tbsp lemon juice, 2 tsp Kashmiri chili powder, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp garam masala, 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste Masala sauce: 2 onions (finely chopped), 400g crushed tomatoes, 200ml heavy cream, 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 2 tsp Kashmiri chili powder, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp garam masala, 1 tsp sugar, 3 tbsp butter, 2 tbsp neutral oil, 1 tbsp dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi — essential for authentic flavor), salt

Instructions:
1. Chicken Tikka: Combine yogurt, lemon juice, and all tikka spices. Add chicken, coat thoroughly. Marinate at least 2 hours (overnight preferred).
2. Chicken Tikka: Preheat grill or broiler to maximum. Cook chicken close to heat, turning once, until charred on edges and cooked through, about 12–15 minutes.
3. Chicken Tikka: Let rest 5 minutes. Cut into 4cm chunks.
4. Masala Sauce: Heat butter and oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add onion. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring often, until very deep golden — 20–25 minutes. This lengthy caramelization is the flavor base.
5. Masala Sauce: Add ginger-garlic paste. Cook 2 minutes.
6. Masala Sauce: Add all spices and sugar. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
7. Masala Sauce: Add crushed tomatoes. Cook 15 minutes until sauce is very thick and oil separates at the edges (bhunao technique — cook until water evaporates from tomatoes).
8. Masala Sauce: Add cream. Stir and simmer 5 minutes.
9. Masala Sauce: Add charred chicken tikka pieces. Simmer 5–10 minutes until chicken is heated through and sauce coats everything.
10. Masala Sauce: Crush dried fenugreek leaves between palms (releases oils). Stir into the sauce.
11. Masala Sauce: Adjust salt and seasoning. Serve with basmati rice or naan.

## Duck Confit
Cuisine: French Gascon | Technique: confit method | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 1 day curing + 3 hrs cooking | Cook: 3 hrs

Ingredients: 4 duck legs, 1 kg duck fat (or enough to submerge legs), 20g coarse salt, 5g sugar, 4 cloves garlic (smashed), 4 sprigs thyme, 2 bay leaves, 10 black peppercorns, 2 juniper berries (crushed), optional: pinch curing salt

Instructions:
1. Day 1 — Cure: Mix salt, sugar, thyme, garlic, peppercorn, and juniper.
2. Day 1 — Cure: Rub duck legs thoroughly with the cure mixture.
3. Day 1 — Cure: Pack into a non-reactive container. Cover with any remaining cure.
4. Day 1 — Cure: Refrigerate uncovered for 24 hours (this draws out moisture and seasons deeply).
5. Day 2 — Cook: Preheat oven to 225°F (107°C).
6. Day 2 — Cook: Rinse cure off duck legs. Pat completely dry.
7. Day 2 — Cook: Melt duck fat in an oven-safe pot or Dutch oven. Heat to 190°F (88°C) — use thermometer.
8. Day 2 — Cook: Submerge duck legs in fat. They should be completely covered.
9. Day 2 — Cook: Transfer to oven. Cook 2.5–3 hours until meat is very tender and separates easily from the bone. Do not let fat exceed 200°F (93°C) — we're poaching in fat, not frying.
10. Day 2 — Cook: Cool in the fat. Can be stored submerged in fat in the refrigerator for weeks.
11. To serve: Remove legs from fat. Scrape off excess fat.
12. To serve: Heat a skillet over medium-high heat (no oil needed — fat renders from skin).

## Jerk Chicken
Cuisine: Jamaican | Technique: marinating + smoking/grilling | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Serves: 4–6 | Prep: 20 min + overnight marinating | Cook: 1 hr

Ingredients: 1 whole chicken (cut in pieces) or 8 chicken thighs Jerk paste: 4–6 Scotch bonnet chilis (seeds removed for less heat), 1 bunch scallions, 6 cloves garlic, 2-inch piece ginger, juice of 2 limes, 3 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tbsp brown sugar, 2 tbsp neutral oil, 2 tsp allspice (ground), 2 tsp thyme (dried or fresh), 1 tsp cinnamon, 1 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp nutmeg, 1 tsp salt

Instructions:
1. Blend all jerk paste ingredients in a food processor to a rough paste.
2. Score chicken pieces deeply with a knife (helps marinade penetrate).
3. Coat chicken thoroughly in jerk paste. Get it into all the cuts.
4. Marinate overnight in the refrigerator, covered.
5. Remove chicken from refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking.
6. Grill method (traditional): Set up two-zone grill: hot side and cooler indirect side. Ideally burn pimento wood (allspice wood) for authentic flavor.
7. Grill method (traditional): Grill chicken on direct heat first to char and mark, 5 minutes per side.
8. Grill method (traditional): Move to indirect heat. Cover and cook 30–40 minutes until internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) for thighs.
9. Oven method: Roast 40–50 minutes until deeply colored and cooked through.
10. Oven method: Rest 10 minutes. Serve with festival (fried dumplings), rice and peas, and coleslaw. Heat note: Scotch bonnet is extremely hot. Taste the paste before marinating. Habanero is a good substitute; jalapeño gives mild heat.

## Korean Bulgogi
Cuisine: Korean | Technique: marinating + grilling or pan-frying | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min + 2 hrs marinating | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 600g ribeye or sirloin (very thinly sliced — ask butcher or partially freeze to slice thin), Marinade: 1/2 Asian pear or kiwi (grated — tenderizes), 4 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tbsp sesame oil, 2 tbsp sugar, 4 cloves garlic (minced), 1 tbsp ginger (grated), 2 scallions (sliced), 2 tbsp mirin, 1 tbsp sesame seeds, 1 tsp black pepper Serve with: steamed rice, lettuce wraps, ssämjang, perilla leaves, kimchi

Instructions:
1. Slice beef as thin as possible (2–3mm). If frozen for 30 minutes, much easier to slice.
2. Combine all marinade ingredients. Add beef. Massage marinade into the meat.
3. Marinate at least 1 hour (2–4 hours ideal). Do not marinate more than 6 hours — the enzyme in pear/kiwi will break down the meat too much.
4. Heat a cast iron grill pan or outdoor grill to very high heat. Alternatively: use a dry nonstick pan over highest heat.
5. Cook bulgogi in batches — do not crowd the pan (steams instead of sears).
6. Cook 1–2 minutes per batch, tossing, until caramelized at the edges.
7. Serve immediately over rice or as ssäm (lettuce wraps): wrap rice, bulgogi, garlic, perilla leaf, and ssämjang in a lettuce cup. Eat in one bite.
