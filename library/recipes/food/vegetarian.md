# Vegetarian and Vegan Mains

## Chana Masala
Cuisine: Punjabi | Technique: simmering | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 15 min (+ overnight soak) | Cook: 1 hr

Ingredients: 400g dried chickpeas (or 2 × 400g cans), 2 large onions (finely chopped), 400g canned tomatoes (or 4 fresh ripe tomatoes, chopped), 4 cloves garlic (minced), 1 tbsp ginger (grated), 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 bay leaf, 1 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp coriander, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp garam masala, 1 tsp amchur (dried mango powder) or 1 tbsp lemon juice, 1/2 tsp chili powder, 2 tbsp oil or ghee, salt, fresh cilantro, 1 tsp chaat masala to finish

Instructions:
1. Soak dried chickpeas overnight. Cook in fresh water 40–60 minutes until tender. Drain, reserving 1 cup cooking liquid. (If using canned: drain and rinse.)
2. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf. Sizzle 30 seconds.
3. Add onion. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring often, for 20–25 minutes until deeply golden (masala base requires well-cooked onions — pale onions = pale masala).
4. Add garlic and ginger. Cook 2 minutes.
5. Add turmeric, coriander, cumin, and chili powder. Cook 1 minute.
6. Add tomatoes. Cook, mashing with a spoon, 10–15 minutes until oil separates from the sides of the masala (bhunao). This means the water has evaporated and flavor is concentrated.
7. Add cooked chickpeas and 1 cup cooking liquid (or 1 cup water for canned).
8. Simmer 15 minutes. Mash some chickpeas against the side to thicken the sauce.
9. Add garam masala and amchur. Simmer 5 more minutes.
10. Taste aggressively — it needs salt, lemon, and possibly more garam masala.
11. Finish with chaat masala. Garnish with cilantro.
12. Serve with bhatura, puri, or rice. A classic combo is Chole Bhature. RATATOUILLE (Confit Byaldi Method — Pixar Method)

## Mapo Tofu
Cuisine: Sichuanese | Technique: simmering | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 600g soft silken tofu (water-packed), 200g ground pork (optional — omit for veg), 2 tbsp doubanjiang (spicy bean paste — the soul of this dish), 1 tbsp black bean sauce, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp Sichuan pepper (toasted and ground), 1 tbsp chili oil, 4 cloves garlic (minced), 1 tbsp ginger (minced), 300ml chicken or vegetable stock, 1 tbsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp cold water, 2 scallions (sliced), 2 tbsp neutral oil

Instructions:
1. Drain tofu gently. Cut into 2–3cm cubes. Handle carefully — silken tofu breaks easily.
2. Bring a pot of lightly salted water to a gentle simmer. Add tofu cubes. Simmer 2–3 minutes to heat through and firm slightly. Drain carefully.
3. Heat oil in a wok over medium-high heat.
4. Add pork (if using). Stir-fry, breaking up clumps, until browned, 3 minutes.
5. Add garlic and ginger. Stir-fry 30 seconds.
6. Add doubanjiang and black bean sauce. Stir-fry in the oil 2 minutes until the oil turns a brilliant red and the paste is very fragrant (hong you — red oil).
7. Add stock. Bring to a simmer.
8. Slide tofu carefully into the sauce. Spoon sauce over the tofu gently. Simmer 3–4 minutes without stirring too much.
9. Add soy sauce and chili oil. Taste and adjust.
10. Stir cornstarch mixture. Pour in half of it — stir very gently. Add more if needed to thicken to a loose, saucy consistency that coats the tofu.
11. Transfer to a bowl. Scatter Sichuan pepper, scallions, and extra chili oil over top.
12. Serve with steamed rice. The numbing heat from Sichuan pepper is non-negotiable. CHANA MASALA (Punjabi Chickpea Curry)

## Palak Paneer
Cuisine: Punjabi | Technique: simmering | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 25 min

Ingredients: 500g paneer (cubed), 500g fresh spinach (or frozen), 1 large onion (roughly chopped), 400g canned tomatoes, 3 cloves garlic, 1 tbsp ginger, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp garam masala, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp chili powder, 100ml heavy cream, 2 tbsp ghee or oil, salt

Instructions:
1. Blanch spinach 1 minute in boiling water. Drain. Transfer immediately to ice water. Squeeze out ALL excess water. Blend to a smooth purée. Set aside.
2. Fry paneer cubes in 1 tbsp ghee over medium-high heat, turning occasionally, until golden on most sides. Remove and set aside on paper towel. (This step optional but gives the paneer a better texture — paneer soaks up sauce better when seared.)
3. In the same pan, heat remaining ghee. Add cumin seeds. Sizzle 30 seconds.
4. Add onion. Cook over medium heat until golden — 10–12 minutes.
5. Add garlic and ginger (paste or minced). Cook 2 minutes.
6. Add all dry spices. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
7. Add tomatoes. Cook 10 minutes until oil separates.
8. Add spinach purée. Stir to combine. Simmer 5 minutes.
9. Add cream. Stir gently.
10. Add fried paneer. Simmer 5 minutes. Season with salt.
11. Serve with naan or basmati rice. Finish with a swirl of cream for presentation.

## Ratatouille
Cuisine: Provençal | Technique: confit vegetables (tian method) or rough method | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 6 | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 1.5 hrs

Ingredients: 2 zucchini, 2 yellow squash, 4 Roma tomatoes, 2 red bell peppers, 2 eggplants (small) Pipérade base: 3 bell peppers (mixed colors), 4 Roma tomatoes, 1 onion, 4 garlic cloves, 4 tbsp olive oil, fresh thyme, fresh basil Vinaigrette: 3 tbsp EVOO, 1 tsp balsamic vinegar, 1 clove garlic, 1 tsp fresh thyme, salt

Instructions:
1. Pipérade base: Finely dice peppers, tomatoes, and onion for the sauce. Mince garlic.
2. Pipérade base: Heat olive oil over medium heat. Cook onion 10 minutes until soft.
3. Pipérade base: Add peppers. Cook 10 minutes. Add garlic. Cook 2 minutes. Add tomatoes.
4. Pipérade base: Simmer 20 minutes until thick. Season and add herbs. Spread evenly in a wide baking dish.
5. Confit vegetable rounds: Using a mandoline or very sharp knife, slice zucchini, squash, tomatoes, and eggplant into very thin rounds (3mm) — all the same diameter if possible.
6. Confit vegetable rounds: Preheat oven to 275°F (135°C).
7. Confit vegetable rounds: Arrange vegetable slices in overlapping concentric circles on top of the pipérade — alternating: tomato, zucchini, squash, eggplant, repeat. Create a beautiful spiral.
8. Confit vegetable rounds: Whisk vinaigrette. Brush generously over all the vegetable slices.
9. Confit vegetable rounds: Place a round of parchment directly on top of the vegetables.
10. Confit vegetable rounds: Bake 1.5–2 hours until vegetables are completely tender and deeply flavored.
11. Confit vegetable rounds: Remove parchment in last 10 minutes to let tops caramelize.
12. Confit vegetable rounds: Rest and serve warm or at room temperature. Even better the next day. Note: Thomas Keller's interpretation for Pixar's Ratatouille. The classic rough Provençal version is faster: chop and sauté all vegetables separately, combine.
