# Soups, Stews and Broths

## Clam Chowder (Manhattan)
Cuisine: American | Technique: simmering | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 6 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 40 min

Ingredients: 1 kg littleneck clams (or 500g canned clams + 500ml clam juice), 150g bacon (diced), 1 large onion (diced), 3 celery stalks (diced), 2 garlic cloves (minced), 3 tbsp all-purpose flour, 500ml chicken or fish stock, 250ml heavy cream, 500g Yukon Gold potatoes (cubed), 2 bay leaves, 1 tsp thyme, salt, white pepper, fresh parsley, oyster crackers or crusty bread

Instructions:
1. If using fresh clams: steam in 1 cup water in a covered pot until just opened, 3–5 minutes. Remove clams from shells, chop roughly. Reserve steaming liquid.
2. Cook bacon in a large pot over medium heat until crisp. Remove bacon, leave fat.
3. Add onion and celery to bacon fat. Cook until softened, 7 minutes. Add garlic, 1 minute.
4. Sprinkle flour over vegetables. Stir to coat. Cook 2 minutes to cook out raw flour.
5. Add reserved clam juice (and stock if needed) to reach 750ml total liquid. Stir vigorously to prevent lumps.
6. Add potatoes, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring to a simmer. Cook 15 minutes until potatoes are just tender.
7. Add cream. Simmer 5 minutes (do not boil hard — cream will break).
8. Add clams. Heat through, 2 minutes. Clams are already cooked — don't overcook.
9. Season with salt and white pepper. Remove bay leaves.
10. Serve in bowls topped with crispy bacon, fresh parsley, and oyster crackers. KIMCHI JJIGAE (Korean Kimchi Stew)

## French Onion Soup
Cuisine: French | Technique: long caramelization, broiling | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Serves: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 1 hr 30 min

Ingredients: 1.5 kg yellow onions (thinly sliced), 4 tbsp unsalted butter, 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp sugar, 1/2 cup dry white wine or sherry, 1.5L good beef broth, 2 bay leaves, 4 sprigs fresh thyme, salt and pepper Gratiné: 8 slices baguette (1 inch thick), 150g Gruyère (grated), 50g Parmesan (grated)

Instructions:
1. Melt butter with olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
2. Add all sliced onions and a generous pinch of salt. Stir to coat in fat.
3. Cover and cook 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until onions have collapsed and softened.
4. Remove lid. Add sugar. Increase heat to medium-high. Continue cooking, stirring every 3–5 minutes, for 45–60 minutes. The onions must go through golden → deep amber → mahogany. Do not rush this. Deglaze with a splash of water if onions stick.
5. Once deeply caramelized, add wine or sherry. Stir and scrape up all fond. Cook until wine evaporates, about 3 minutes.
6. Add broth, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook 20 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Remove bay and thyme.
7. Preheat broiler. Place baguette slices on a tray and toast on both sides.
8. Ladle hot soup into oven-safe bowls. Lay toasted baguette on top.
9. Pile Gruyère and Parmesan generously over the bread and allow it to fall into the soup.
10. Place bowls on a baking tray under the broiler for 3–5 minutes until cheese is bubbling, spotted brown, and starting to char at edges. Serve immediately.

## Gazpacho
Cuisine: Spanish (Andalusian) | Technique: cold blending | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan | Serves: 4–6 | Prep: 20 min + 2 hrs chilling | Cook: 0 min

Ingredients: 1 kg very ripe tomatoes, 1 cucumber (peeled and seeded), 1 red bell pepper (seeded), 1/2 small red onion, 2 cloves garlic, 100ml excellent EVOO, 3 tbsp sherry vinegar, 2 thick slices day-old white bread (crusts removed, soaked in water), salt, cold water Garnish: diced cucumber, tomato, and pepper; croutons; EVOO drizzle

Instructions:
1. Roughly chop tomatoes, cucumber, pepper, and onion.
2. Squeeze bread to remove excess water.
3. Blend all vegetables, bread, and garlic in batches until completely smooth. If using a standard blender: blend 3 minutes per batch.
4. With blender running, slowly pour in olive oil and sherry vinegar.
5. Strain through a medium sieve, pressing solids. For smoother texture, strain twice.
6. Season generously with salt. Add cold water to achieve desired consistency — gazpacho should pour easily but not be watery.
7. Refrigerate at least 2 hours (overnight is better — flavors meld dramatically).
8. Taste and re-season before serving (cold dulls salt and acid).
9. Serve in chilled bowls or glasses over ice. Garnish with brunoise vegetables, croutons, and a thread of olive oil. CLAM CHOWDER (New England)

## Harira
Cuisine: Moroccan | Technique: simmering | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 6–8 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 1 hr

Ingredients: 300g lamb or beef (cubed small), 400g canned chickpeas, 200g brown or green lentils, 400g canned tomatoes, 1 large onion (grated), 3 tbsp tomato paste, large bunch parsley and cilantro (chopped), 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp ginger, 1 tsp cinnamon, 1 tsp pepper, 1/2 tsp saffron (steeped in 2 tbsp warm water), 2 tbsp olive oil, 2 tbsp smen (preserved butter) or regular butter, juice of 1 lemon, 60g vermicelli, 2 eggs (beaten), 2 tbsp flour dissolved in water (tedouira — thickener), salt

Instructions:
1. Heat oil and butter in a large pot over medium heat.
2. Add meat, onion, tomato paste, and all spices. Stir to coat and cook 5 minutes.
3. Add fresh tomatoes, half the parsley and cilantro, lentils, and saffron water. Add 2 liters of water. Bring to a boil.
4. Skim any foam. Reduce heat and simmer 30 minutes.
5. Add chickpeas. Cook another 15 minutes until lentils are very soft and starting to break down.
6. Add vermicelli. Cook 5 minutes.
7. Stir flour-water mixture into the soup while stirring constantly to prevent lumps.
8. With soup at a vigorous simmer, pour in beaten eggs in a thin stream while stirring continuously — they will cook into fine threads.
9. Add lemon juice, remaining cilantro and parsley. Season with salt.
10. Serve with dates, chebbakia (honey pastry), and crusty bread. Note: Traditional Ramadan dish served at Iftar (breaking of fast). The soup is deeply nourishing and filling — meant to revive after a day of fasting. GROUNDNUT SOUP / MAAFE (West African Peanut Stew)

## Kimchi Jjigae
Cuisine: Korean | Technique: simmering | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 2–3 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 30 min

Ingredients: 300g well-fermented kimchi (older kimchi = better flavor), 200g pork belly (sliced thin), 200g soft or firm tofu (cubed), 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes), 1 tbsp sesame oil, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sugar, 500ml anchovy stock or water, 2 scallions (cut in 2cm lengths), 2 cloves garlic (minced), cooked rice to serve

Instructions:
1. Cut kimchi into 2-inch pieces. Reserve any kimchi juice — it's liquid gold for this dish.
2. In a medium pot or Korean earthenware pot (ttukbaegi), add sesame oil over medium heat.
3. Add pork belly slices. Cook until lightly browned, about 3–4 minutes.
4. Add garlic and gochugaru. Stir-fry 30 seconds.
5. Add kimchi and any reserved kimchi juice. Stir and cook 3 minutes.
6. Add stock or water. Bring to a boil.
7. Reduce heat to a vigorous simmer. Cook 15–20 minutes. The longer it simmers, the more the kimchi flavor deepens and the pork becomes tender.
8. Add tofu cubes gently. Cook 5 more minutes without stirring (tofu breaks easily).
9. Add soy sauce, sugar, and scallions. Taste and adjust.
10. Serve in the cooking pot at the table, still bubbling, with steamed rice. HARIRA (Moroccan Ramadan Soup)

## Lobster Bisque
Cuisine: French-American | Technique: simmering + blending | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4–6 | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 1 hr

Ingredients: 2 whole lobsters (or 4 lobster tails + 2 cups lobster shell stock), 3 tbsp butter, 1 onion (diced), 2 carrots (diced), 2 celery stalks (diced), 4 garlic cloves (minced), 3 tbsp tomato paste, 100ml cognac or brandy, 200ml dry white wine, 1L seafood or fish stock, 300ml heavy cream, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp dried tarragon, salt, white pepper, 2 tbsp fresh chives (to garnish)

Instructions:
1. Split and cook lobsters: Split in half lengthwise. Season. Roast at 425°F 12–15 min. Remove meat. Save all shells and juices.
2. Chop shells roughly. In a large pot over high heat, toast shells in butter until bright red.
3. Add onion, carrot, and celery. Cook 5 minutes.
4. Add garlic and tomato paste. Cook 3 minutes until paste darkens.
5. Add cognac — flambé if desired or just cook off the alcohol, 2 minutes.
6. Add wine. Reduce by half.
7. Add stock and cream. Add paprika and tarragon. Simmer 30 minutes.
8. Strain through a fine mesh strainer. Press shells to extract all liquid.
9. Return bisque to pot. Bring to simmer. Taste and season with salt and white pepper.
10. Cut reserved lobster meat into chunks. Add to bisque to warm, 2 minutes.
11. Ladle into warm bowls. Garnish with chives and a drizzle of cream. CASSOULET (Simplified French Home Version)

## Minestrone
Cuisine: Italian | Technique: simmering | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 6 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 45 min

Ingredients: 3 tbsp olive oil, 1 onion (diced), 2 carrots (diced), 2 celery stalks (diced), 4 garlic cloves (minced), 400g canned chopped tomatoes, 1.5L vegetable or chicken broth, 400g canned cannellini beans (drained), 150g small pasta (ditalini, macaroni, or orzo), 1 zucchini (diced), 2 cups kale or Savoy cabbage (shredded), 1 cup green beans (cut), 1 Parmesan rind (optional but transformative), salt, pepper, fresh basil, EVOO to finish

Instructions:
1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, and celery. Cook, stirring, until softened and lightly golden, about 8 minutes.
2. Add garlic. Cook 1 minute.
3. Add tomatoes, broth, and Parmesan rind (if using). Bring to a boil.
4. Add green beans and zucchini. Simmer 10 minutes.
5. Add cannellini beans and cabbage or kale. Simmer another 10 minutes.
6. Add pasta. Cook until pasta is tender, about 8–10 minutes.
7. Remove Parmesan rind. Season generously with salt and pepper.
8. Serve in bowls drizzled with excellent olive oil, torn fresh basil, and grated Parmesan. Note: Minestrone improves significantly the next day. Add pasta fresh when reheating. PHO BO (Vietnamese Beef Pho)

## Miso Soup (basic)
Cuisine: Japanese | Technique: dissolving/no boiling | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Very Easy | Diet: vegan | Serves: 4 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 1L dashi (see below) or water + 1 tbsp dashi granules, 3–4 tbsp white or mixed miso, 200g soft tofu (cubed), 2 tbsp dried wakame seaweed, 2 scallions (thinly sliced) Dashi: 1L cold water, 10g kombu, 20g katsuobushi (bonito flakes)

Instructions:
1. Dashi: Soak kombu in cold water for 30 minutes (or overnight in fridge for deeper flavor).
2. Dashi: Slowly heat to just below a simmer (~60°C/140°F). Remove kombu.
3. Dashi: Bring to a gentle simmer. Add katsuobushi all at once.
4. Dashi: Turn off heat immediately. Let steep 5 minutes.
5. Dashi: Strain through fine mesh. Do not squeeze the bonito (makes dashi bitter).
6. Miso Soup: Soak wakame in cold water 5 minutes until rehydrated. Drain.
7. Miso Soup: Heat dashi in a pot over medium heat to just below a simmer (do not boil miso).
8. Miso Soup: Place miso in a ladle or small bowl. Add a splash of warm dashi and whisk to dissolve.
9. Miso Soup: Stir dissolved miso into the pot. Taste for seasoning.
10. Miso Soup: Add tofu and wakame. Heat 1 minute without boiling.
11. Miso Soup: Serve immediately, topped with scallions. Critical note: Never boil miso — it kills the beneficial bacteria and ruins the flavor.

## Pho Bo (Beef Pho)
Cuisine: Vietnamese | Technique: long simmering | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 4–6 hrs (broth)

Ingredients: Broth: 2 kg beef bones (knuckles, marrow), 500g beef brisket, 1 large onion (halved), 8cm ginger (halved), 4 star anise, 2 cinnamon sticks, 5 cloves, 3 tbsp fish sauce, 1 tbsp sugar, salt To serve: 400g dried flat rice noodles (bánh phở), 200g beef sirloin (paper-thin sliced), bean sprouts, Thai basil, fresh lime, sliced chili, hoisin, sriracha, sliced scallions

Instructions:
1. Bring a large pot of water to boil. Blanch beef bones and brisket for 5 minutes. Drain, rinse bones thoroughly under cold water to remove impurities.
2. Char onion and ginger: place cut-side down directly over a gas flame or under a very hot broiler until deeply charred, about 5 minutes. This is critical for flavor.
3. Toast star anise, cinnamon, and cloves in a dry pan over medium heat until fragrant, 2 minutes. Bundle in cheesecloth or a spice strainer.
4. Add blanched bones, brisket, charred onion and ginger, and spice bundle to a large stockpot. Cover with cold water by 2 inches (about 4–5 liters).
5. Bring to a simmer. Skim foam diligently for the first 20–30 minutes.
6. Add fish sauce and sugar. Reduce heat to a very gentle simmer. Cook 4–6 hours. The broth should be gently simmering — large bubbles cloud the broth.
7. Remove brisket after 1.5–2 hours (when tender). Slice for serving.
8. Strain broth through a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth. Season with fish sauce and salt.
9. Soak rice noodles in cold water 30 minutes. Boil briefly (1–2 min) before serving.
10. In each bowl: place cooked noodles, sliced brisket, and a few paper-thin raw sirloin slices.
11. Ladle very hot broth over — this cooks the raw sirloin. The broth must be at a rolling boil.
12. Serve immediately with full plate of garnishes — bean sprouts, basil, lime, chili, hoisin, and sriracha on the side. TOM YUM GOONG (Thai Hot and Sour Shrimp Soup)

## Pozole Rojo
Cuisine: Mexican | Technique: long simmering | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 8 | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 3 hrs

Ingredients: 1 kg pork shoulder (cut in large pieces), 800g hominy (canned, drained), 4 dried ancho chilis, 3 dried guajillo chilis, 1/2 dried pasilla chili, 1 onion (halved), 6 garlic cloves, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp dried oregano, 2L chicken or pork stock, salt Garnishes: shredded cabbage, thinly sliced radishes, diced white onion, dried oregano, dried chili flakes, fresh lime, tostadas, sliced avocado, sour cream

Instructions:
1. Brown pork pieces in oil on all sides. Remove.
2. Simmer pork in stock with halved onion, 3 garlic cloves, and salt for 1.5–2 hours until very tender. Remove pork and shred. Reserve the broth.
3. Toast dried chilis in a dry pan 30 seconds per side (no longer — burns fast = bitter).
4. Stem and seed chilis. Soak in boiling water 20 minutes.
5. Blend soaked chilis with remaining garlic, cumin, oregano, and 1 cup soaking liquid until completely smooth. Strain through medium mesh.
6. Heat 2 tbsp oil in the pot. Add chili purée — it will spit. Cook, stirring, 3–5 minutes.
7. Add reserved broth. Add hominy. Add shredded pork.
8. Simmer 30 minutes to meld flavors. Taste and season generously with salt.
9. Serve in large bowls with ALL the garnishes on the table — pozole is about the garnishes. Each person customizes their bowl: shredded cabbage, radishes, onion, lime, oregano, tostada for crunch, avocado, sour cream. SEARED SCALLOPS with Pea Purée

## Tom Yum Goong
Cuisine: Thai | Technique: simmering | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 500g large shrimp (heads on preferred, peeled and deveined), 1.2L chicken stock, 3 stalks lemongrass (bruised, cut in 3), 5 slices galangal (or ginger), 6 kaffir lime leaves (torn), 200g mushrooms (straw or oyster, halved), 3 tbsp fish sauce, 3 tbsp lime juice, 2 tsp palm sugar or sugar, 4–6 Thai bird's eye chilis (bruised), 2 tbsp Nam Prik Pao (roasted chili paste — optional but traditional), fresh cilantro, scallions

Instructions:
1. Bring stock to a boil in a medium pot.
2. Add lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves. Simmer 5 minutes to infuse.
3. Add mushrooms. Cook 3 minutes.
4. Add shrimp and bruised chilis. Cook just until shrimp turns pink and curls, about 2–3 minutes. Do not overcook.
5. Remove from heat. Stir in fish sauce, lime juice, sugar, and Nam Prik Pao.
6. Taste and balance: should be hot (chili), sour (lime), salty (fish sauce), and slightly sweet. Adjust accordingly.
7. Ladle into bowls. Top with fresh cilantro and scallions. Note: The lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime are aromatics — not meant to be eaten. Leave them in the soup for presentation but warn guests.

## Tonkotsu Ramen
Cuisine: Japanese | Technique: 12-hr simmering | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Hard | Serves: 4 | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 12+ hrs

Ingredients: Broth: 2 kg pork trotters and neck bones, 300g chicken wings, 2L water Tare (seasoning): 100ml soy sauce, 50ml mirin, 50ml sake, 2 tsp salt Chashu pork: 500g pork belly, 100ml soy, 100ml mirin, 100ml sake, 50ml water, 2 tbsp sugar Ramen eggs (ajitsuke tamago): 6 eggs, leftover chashu braising liquid Toppings: bamboo shoots (menma), nori sheets, corn, butter, scallions, sesame, togarashi 400g fresh or dried ramen noodles

Instructions:
1. Chashu Pork (make first): Roll pork belly tightly and tie with kitchen twine at 2cm intervals.
2. Chashu Pork (make first): Sear in oil on all sides until golden, about 8 minutes.
3. Chashu Pork (make first): Add soy, mirin, sake, water, and sugar. Bring to boil, reduce to very gentle simmer. Cover and cook 2 hours, turning every 30 minutes.
4. Chashu Pork (make first): Cool in the liquid. Refrigerate overnight — slice when cold for clean cuts.
5. Ramen Eggs: Boil eggs exactly 6.5 minutes from cold water. Transfer immediately to ice bath for 5 min.
6. Ramen Eggs: Peel carefully. Marinate in chashu cooking liquid diluted with water (1:1) for 6–24 hours.
7. Tonkotsu Broth: Blanch pork bones in boiling water 5 minutes. Drain and rinse thoroughly.
8. Tonkotsu Broth: Return to a large pot. Cover with fresh water. Bring to a VIGOROUS boil. Skim foam for 10 minutes.
9. Tonkotsu Broth: Maintain a rolling boil (unlike standard stocks — the aggressive boiling emulsifies fat and creates the characteristic white, milky broth). Cook 10–12 hours, adding water to maintain level. The broth should turn completely white and creamy.
10. Tonkotsu Broth: Add chicken wings in last 2 hours for additional richness.
11. Tonkotsu Broth: Strain through fine mesh. Season with tare — start with 2 tbsp per bowl, adjust.
12. Assembly: Cook noodles per package instructions. Drain well.

Cuisine: Provençal | Technique: simmering | Time: 30 min | Diet: gluten-free-ish
