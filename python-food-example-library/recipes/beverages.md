# Beverages and Drinks

## Espresso Martini
Cuisine: British modern (Dick Bradsell) | Technique: shaking | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 1 | Prep: 3 min

Ingredients: 40ml vodka, 30ml espresso (freshly made and cooled 5 min), 20ml coffee liqueur (Kahlúa or Tia Maria), 10ml simple syrup (optional — depends on liqueur sweetness)

Instructions:
1. Pull a fresh shot of espresso. Cool 5 minutes.
2. Chill a coupe glass in the freezer.
3. Combine vodka, espresso, coffee liqueur, and simple syrup in a cocktail shaker.
4. Fill with ice (the larger the better for good dilution).
5. Shake VIGOROUSLY for 20+ seconds — harder than any other cocktail. The vigorous shaking is what creates the signature thick foam from the espresso crema.
6. Double strain (through cocktail strainer AND fine mesh) into chilled coupe glass.
7. The crema foam should sit about 5mm high. Garnish with 3 espresso beans. Key: This cocktail MUST be drunk immediately — the foam collapses within 5 minutes. SUPPLEMENTAL INSTRUCTIONS: KEY REMAINING RECIPES POKE BOWL (Hawaiian Tuna)

## Horchata
Cuisine: Mexican | Technique: soaking + blending + straining | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 6–8 | Prep: 10 min + overnight soaking | Cook: 0

Ingredients: 1 cup long-grain white rice, 3/4 cup blanched almonds, 2 cinnamon sticks, 4 cups cold water (for soaking), 3 cups cold water (for blending/serving), 1/2 cup sugar, 1 tsp vanilla extract, additional cold water and ice to serve

Instructions:
1. Rinse rice. Combine rice, almonds, and cinnamon sticks in a bowl.
2. Add 4 cups cold water. Cover and soak overnight at room temperature (or refrigerator for 8 hrs).
3. Remove cinnamon sticks.
4. Transfer soaked rice and almonds with their soaking liquid to a blender.
5. Blend on highest setting for 3–4 minutes until as smooth as possible.
6. Strain through a fine mesh strainer, then through a cheesecloth or nut milk bag. Squeeze every last drop — the liquid is your horchata.
7. Stir in sugar and vanilla until sugar dissolves.
8. Add 3 cups cold water to achieve desired consistency. Taste and adjust sweetness.
9. Refrigerate until cold. Stir before serving over ice. MULLED WINE (Glühwein)

## Margarita
Cuisine: Mexican | Technique: shaking or blending | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 1 | Prep: 5 min

Ingredients: 50ml 100% agave tequila (blanco), 25ml Cointreau or triple sec, 25ml fresh lime juice (MUST be fresh — never bottled), salt for rim, lime wedge

Instructions:
1. Run a lime wedge around the rim of a rocks glass (outside edge only, not the inside). Dip in fine salt to coat half the rim.
2. Combine tequila, Cointreau, and lime juice in a cocktail shaker. Fill with ice.
3. Shake vigorously for 15 seconds — a well-chilled margarita is a cold margarita.
4. Strain over fresh ice (rocks glass) or straight up into a coupe.
5. Garnish with a lime wheel on the rim. Variation: Spicy margarita — muddle 2–3 jalapeño slices before adding spirits.

## Masala Chai
Cuisine: Indian | Technique: simmering spices + tea | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 500ml whole milk, 500ml water, 4 tsp strong black tea (Assam or Darjeeling — loose leaf), 4cm fresh ginger (grated or sliced thin), 4 green cardamom pods (lightly crushed), 1 cinnamon stick, 4 cloves, 4 black peppercorns, 2 tbsp sugar (or to taste)

Instructions:
1. Combine water, ginger, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and peppercorns in a small saucepan.
2. Bring to a boil. Simmer 5 minutes to infuse spices.
3. Add tea leaves. Simmer 2 minutes.
4. Add milk. Bring back to a simmer.
5. Add sugar. Taste and adjust sweetness.
6. Bring to a full boil briefly (this melds milk and tea).
7. Strain through a fine mesh strainer into cups.
8. Traditionally served hot, often poured between two cups from a height to create froth. HORCHATA (Mexican Rice Milk Drink)

## Mulled Wine (Glühwein)
Cuisine: German/British | Technique: hot infusing | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Very Easy | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 8 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 750ml red wine (inexpensive but drinkable Merlot or Shiraz), 2 cinnamon sticks, 6 cloves, 2 star anise, 3 cardamom pods (cracked), 1 orange (sliced in rounds), 50ml brandy, 3–4 tbsp honey or brown sugar (to taste)

Instructions:
1. Combine all ingredients except brandy in a medium saucepan.
2. Heat over medium-low heat until steaming — do NOT boil (alcohol evaporates and boiling makes wine bitter and harsh).
3. Simmer very gently, 15–20 minutes, until deeply fragrant.
4. Add brandy. Taste for sweetness.
5. Strain into heat-proof glasses or mugs. Garnish each with a cinnamon stick and an orange slice.

## Negroni
Cuisine: Italian | Technique: stirring | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 1 | Prep: 3 min

Ingredients: 30ml gin, 30ml sweet vermouth (Carpano Antica or Punt e Mes preferred), 30ml Campari, large ice cube or ice sphere, fresh orange peel or orange slice

Instructions:
1. Combine gin, vermouth, and Campari in a mixing glass (or the serving glass itself).
2. Fill with ice. Stir 30–40 rotations — stirred cocktails need more stirring than shaken.
3. Strain into a chilled rocks glass over fresh ice.
4. Express orange peel over the glass, run around the rim, and drop in or perch on edge. Note: 1:1:1 ratio is the classic. Some prefer 1.5:1:1 gin-forward. Experiment. MARGARITA (Classic)

## Old Fashioned
Cuisine: American | Technique: stirring + expressing citrus | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegan | Serves: 1 | Prep: 3 min

Ingredients: 60ml bourbon or rye whiskey, 1 sugar cube (or 1 tsp simple syrup), 2–3 dashes Angostura bitters, large ice cube, orange peel (about 5cm × 2cm)

Instructions:
1. Place sugar cube in a rocks glass. Saturate with 2–3 dashes Angostura bitters. Add 1 tsp water. Muddle until sugar is dissolved.
2. Add a large, clear ice cube (single cube melts slower = less dilution).
3. Pour bourbon over the ice.
4. Stir slowly for 20–30 seconds — not to chill aggressively, but to integrate and dilute properly. The drink should feel silky, not sharp.
5. Express orange peel: hold a strip of orange peel, skin side down, over the glass. Firmly squeeze and twist — oils will spray across the surface of the drink.
6. Run the peel around the rim of the glass. Drop peel in or rest on ice.
7. Serve without garnish embellishment — the simplicity is the point.
