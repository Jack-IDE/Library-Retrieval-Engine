# Breakfast and Brunch Recipes

## Avocado Toast with Poached Egg
Cuisine: Modern | Technique: poaching, toasting | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 1 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 5 min

Ingredients: 2 slices sourdough bread, 1 ripe avocado, 1 lemon, 2 eggs, 1 tbsp white wine vinegar, everything bagel seasoning, red pepper flakes, EVOO, flaky sea salt, black pepper

Instructions:
1. Toast sourdough in toaster or under broiler until golden and crisp.
2. Halve avocado, remove stone. Scoop flesh into a bowl. Add a squeeze of lemon juice, a pinch of salt, and pepper. Mash to your preferred texture — chunky or smooth.
3. Bring a medium saucepan of water to a gentle simmer. Add vinegar.
4. Crack each egg into a small cup. Gently lower each egg into the water. Poach 3 minutes for runny yolk. Remove with slotted spoon onto paper towel.
5. Spread avocado thickly onto toast. Top with poached egg.
6. Drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle everything bagel seasoning, red pepper flakes, and a pinch of flaky salt.

## Bircher Muesli
Cuisine: Swiss | Technique: cold soak | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Very Easy | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 2 | Prep: 5 min + overnight | Cook: 0 min

Ingredients: 1 cup rolled oats, 1/2 cup apple juice, 1/2 cup milk, 1/2 cup plain yogurt, 1 apple (grated), 1 tbsp lemon juice, 2 tbsp honey, 1/4 cup mixed nuts (roughly chopped), 2 tbsp raisins or dried cranberries, fresh berries to serve

Instructions:
1. Combine oats, apple juice, and milk. Stir and refrigerate overnight.
2. In the morning, stir in yogurt, grated apple (with skin), lemon juice, and honey.
3. The mixture will be thick and creamy. Add more milk for looser consistency.
4. Divide into bowls. Top with chopped nuts, dried fruit, and fresh berries. Note: Named after Swiss physician Maximilian Bircher-Benner. The original used very little sweetener — adjust to taste.

## Classic American Pancakes
Cuisine: American | Technique: pan-frying | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 4 (makes ~12 pancakes) | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 2 tsp baking powder, 1/2 tsp baking soda, 1 tsp sugar, 1/2 tsp salt, 2 cups buttermilk, 2 eggs, 3 tbsp melted butter, butter for cooking

Instructions:
1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt.
2. In a separate bowl, whisk together buttermilk, eggs, and melted butter.
3. Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients. Fold gently with a spatula until just combined — lumps are fine and desirable. Do not overmix (activates gluten = tough pancakes).
4. Let batter rest 5 minutes while heating the pan.
5. Heat a griddle or large nonstick pan over medium heat. Melt a small pat of butter.
6. Pour approximately 1/4 cup batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2–3 minutes.
7. Flip once. Cook another 1–2 minutes until golden underneath.
8. Keep warm in a 200°F oven on a baking sheet while finishing the rest.
9. Serve stacked with butter and warm maple syrup.

## Crêpes (French)
Cuisine: French | Technique: very thin pan-frying | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 4 (makes ~12 crêpes) | Prep: 5 min + 30 min rest | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 3 eggs, 2.5 cups whole milk, 3 tbsp melted butter, 1 tbsp sugar (for sweet), pinch salt, butter for cooking

Instructions:
1. Blend flour, eggs, milk, butter, sugar, and salt together — use a blender for silky smooth batter, or whisk vigorously by hand.
2. Rest batter in the refrigerator at least 30 minutes (up to overnight). This relaxes the gluten for tender crêpes.
3. Heat a crêpe pan or 10-inch nonstick pan over medium-high heat. Add a small pat of butter.
4. Pour in approximately 3 tablespoons batter. Immediately tilt and swirl the pan in a circular motion so batter spreads into a thin, even round.
5. Cook until edges turn golden and center looks dry, about 1–1.5 minutes.
6. Flip with a thin spatula (or confidently with fingers). Cook 30 seconds on second side.
7. Slide onto a plate. Stack crêpes — they will not stick.
8. Serve with lemon and sugar, Nutella and banana, jam and cream, or savory fillings. Note: First crêpe is always a test — discard or eat as chef's snack. The pan temperature corrects itself by the second one.

## Dutch Baby
Cuisine: German-American | Technique: oven-puffed baking | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 2–3 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 3 eggs, 3/4 cup whole milk (room temperature), 3/4 cup all-purpose flour, 2 tbsp sugar, 1/2 tsp vanilla, pinch nutmeg, pinch salt, 3 tbsp unsalted butter To serve: powdered sugar, lemon juice, jam, or fruit

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Place a 10-inch cast iron skillet in the oven while it preheats.
2. Blend eggs, milk, flour, sugar, vanilla, nutmeg, and salt together until completely smooth. Rest batter 10 minutes (or make ahead and refrigerate overnight).
3. Carefully remove hot skillet from oven. Add butter — it will melt and foam immediately. Swirl to coat the pan completely.
4. Pour in batter all at once. Return to oven immediately.
5. Bake 18–22 minutes without opening the oven until dramatically puffed and deeply golden at the edges. It will deflate slightly upon removal — this is normal.
6. Dust with powdered sugar. Squeeze lemon over. Serve immediately, straight from the pan.

## Eggs Benedict
Cuisine: American | Technique: poaching, emulsifying | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 2 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 4 eggs, 4 slices Canadian bacon, 2 English muffins, 1 tbsp white vinegar Hollandaise: 3 egg yolks, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 125g clarified butter, salt, cayenne

Instructions:
1. Make hollandaise: Whisk egg yolks and lemon juice in a heatproof bowl over barely simmering water (double boiler). Whisk until thick and doubled in volume (~4 min).
2. Remove from heat. Slowly drizzle in warm clarified butter drop by drop at first, then in a thin stream, whisking constantly until thick and emulsified.
3. Season with salt and cayenne. Keep warm by setting bowl over warm (not hot) water.
4. Bring a wide saucepan of water to a gentle simmer. Add vinegar.
5. Crack each egg into a small cup. Create a gentle swirl in the water with a spoon. Slide egg in. Poach 3–4 minutes until white is set, yolk still runny. Remove with slotted spoon onto paper towel.
6. Meanwhile, pan-fry Canadian bacon in a dry skillet until lightly browned.
7. Split and toast English muffins. Place on plates.
8. Top each muffin half with a slice of Canadian bacon, then a poached egg.
9. Spoon hollandaise generously over each egg. Serve immediately. Note: If hollandaise breaks, whisk a new egg yolk in a clean bowl, then slowly whisk in the broken sauce — it will re-emulsify.

## French Omelette
Cuisine: French | Technique: low-heat folding | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Medium (technique-dependent) | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 1 | Prep: 2 min | Cook: 2 min

Ingredients: 3 eggs, 1 tbsp unsalted butter, pinch of fine salt, 1 tbsp fresh chives or fines herbes

Instructions:
1. Crack eggs into a bowl. Season with a small pinch of salt. Beat vigorously with a fork until completely homogenous — no streaks of white visible.
2. Heat an 8-inch nonstick or well-seasoned carbon steel pan over medium-high heat.
3. Add butter. As soon as it stops foaming and before it colors, pour in the eggs.
4. Immediately begin stirring rapidly with a rubber spatula in small circles while simultaneously shaking the pan. The goal is very fine, tiny curds.
5. As the eggs start to set on the bottom, tilt the pan and use the spatula to fold the near edge of the omelette toward the center.
6. Tap the pan handle firmly — the omelette will slide to the far edge.
7. Tilt the pan over the plate and roll the omelette out, seam-side down, folding as it lands. It should be pale yellow, barely colored, cigar-shaped.
8. Scatter herbs over the top. Serve immediately — omelettes wait for no one. Note: The omelette should take no more than 90 seconds total. Practice is everything. TAMAGOYAKI (Japanese Rolled Omelette)

## Full English Breakfast
Cuisine: British | Technique: multi-method | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Medium (timing) | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 1 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 2 back bacon rashers, 2 pork sausages, 2 eggs, 1 slice black pudding, 1 halved tomato, 2–3 button mushrooms, 1/2 can baked beans (heinz), 2 slices white toast, butter, oil

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 200°F to keep components warm as they finish. Set out a tray.
2. Heat a splash of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Add sausages first — they take the longest (8–10 min). Turn every 2 minutes to brown all sides.
4. Add black pudding slices and bacon after sausages have had 4 minutes. Cook bacon until edges crisp. Flip black pudding once, about 2 min per side.
5. Transfer sausages, bacon, and black pudding to oven tray to keep warm.
6. In the same pan, add tomato halves cut-side down and mushrooms. Cook until tomato is softened and mushrooms are golden, about 3–4 minutes. Add to the tray.
7. Heat baked beans in a small saucepan over low heat.
8. Fry eggs in the pan to your preference (sunny side up or over easy).
9. Toast bread, butter it.
10. Arrange everything on a warm plate: eggs, sausages, bacon, black pudding, tomato, mushrooms, beans, toast. Serve immediately — the full English waits for no one.

## Huevos Rancheros
Cuisine: Mexican | Technique: frying | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 2 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 4 eggs, 4 corn tortillas, 1 cup salsa roja (homemade or store-bought), 1/2 cup black beans (rinsed), 1/4 cup cotija cheese or queso fresco (crumbled), 2 tbsp sour cream or crema, fresh cilantro, lime wedges, hot sauce, neutral oil

Instructions:
1. Warm salsa roja in a small pan over low heat.
2. Warm black beans in a separate small pan with a splash of water. Season with cumin and salt.
3. Heat 1/4 inch oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Fry each tortilla 20 seconds per side until puffed and lightly crisped (not stiff — just lightly fried). Drain on paper towel.
4. In the same pan with residual oil, fry eggs to your liking (sunny-side-up is traditional).
5. Assemble: Place 2 tortillas per plate, overlapping. Spoon warm salsa over tortillas. Add a spoonful of black beans.
6. Lay fried eggs on top of the sauced tortillas.
7. Top with cotija, a drizzle of crema, fresh cilantro, and lime wedges.

## Japanese Soufflé Pancakes
Cuisine: Japanese | Technique: steam-pan method | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Hard | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 2 (makes 4 thick pancakes) | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 2 eggs (separated), 3 tbsp milk, 1/2 tsp vanilla, 1/4 cup + 1 tbsp cake flour, 1/4 tsp baking powder, 3 tbsp sugar (for whites), 1/4 tsp cream of tartar, neutral oil

Instructions:
1. Separate eggs. In a bowl, whisk together yolks, milk, and vanilla.
2. Sift in flour and baking powder. Whisk until smooth batter forms.
3. In a clean bowl, beat egg whites and cream of tartar with electric mixer. Add sugar in three additions while beating. Beat to stiff, glossy peaks.
4. Gently fold 1/3 of meringue into yolk batter to lighten. Fold in remaining meringue in two additions. Stop folding when just combined — keep as much air as possible.
5. Heat a nonstick pan or griddle over very low heat. Brush with oil.
6. Using a large spoon or piping bag, pile batter into thick rounds, stacking two spoons of batter per pancake for height.
7. Cover with a lid. Cook 4–5 minutes until bottoms are golden.
8. Very gently flip using two spatulas. Cover and cook another 4–5 minutes.
9. Serve immediately (they deflate quickly) with whipped cream, syrup, and fruit.

## Okonomiyaki
Cuisine: Japanese | Technique: pan-frying | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 2 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 2 cups finely shredded green cabbage, 1 cup all-purpose flour, 3/4 cup dashi or water, 2 eggs, 4 strips bacon or pork belly (sliced thin), 2 scallions (sliced), Toppings: Kewpie mayo, okonomiyaki sauce (or 3 tbsp Worcestershire + 1 tbsp ketchup), aonori (dried seaweed flakes), katsuobushi (bonito flakes), pickled ginger

Instructions:
1. In a bowl, whisk together flour and dashi until smooth.
2. Add eggs and whisk to combine.
3. Fold in shredded cabbage and half the scallions. The batter should be quite thick.
4. Heat a large nonstick or cast iron pan over medium heat. Add a thin layer of oil.
5. Pour in half the batter, spreading into a thick round ~20cm across.
6. Lay bacon/pork slices over the top. Cook 4–5 minutes until bottom is deeply golden.
7. Flip carefully with two spatulas. Cook another 4–5 minutes until cooked through.
8. Transfer to plate. Repeat with remaining batter.
9. Drizzle okonomiyaki sauce in horizontal lines, then Kewpie mayo in crossing lines.
10. Top generously with aonori, katsuobushi (it will wave in the heat), remaining scallions, and pickled ginger.

## Shakshuka
Cuisine: Middle Eastern/Israeli | Technique: poaching in sauce | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 2–3 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 25 min

Ingredients: 4–6 eggs, 400g crushed San Marzano tomatoes, 1 red bell pepper (diced), 1 onion (diced), 4 cloves garlic (sliced), 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp sweet paprika, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, 1/4 tsp cayenne, 3 tbsp olive oil, salt, pepper, fresh parsley or cilantro, crumbled feta (optional), crusty bread to serve

Instructions:
1. Heat olive oil in a large, deep skillet or cast iron pan over medium heat.
2. Add onion. Cook until softened and translucent, about 7 minutes.
3. Add bell pepper. Cook another 5 minutes until softened.
4. Add garlic, cumin, paprika, smoked paprika, and cayenne. Stir and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
5. Pour in crushed tomatoes. Season generously with salt and pepper. Stir to combine.
6. Simmer over medium-low heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until sauce thickens slightly.
7. Use a spoon to create shallow wells in the sauce. Crack one egg into each well.
8. Cover the pan with a lid. Cook 5–8 minutes until whites are just set but yolks remain runny (check at 5 min — every minute matters here).
9. Remove from heat. Scatter feta and fresh herbs over the top.
10. Serve directly from the pan with crusty bread for dipping.

## Tamagoyaki
Cuisine: Japanese | Technique: rolling layers in rectangular pan | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 2 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 4 eggs, 2 tbsp dashi, 1 tbsp mirin, 1 tsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sugar, neutral oil

Instructions:
1. Whisk together eggs, dashi, mirin, soy sauce, and sugar until smooth. Strain through a fine sieve to remove chalazae for smoothness.
2. Heat a rectangular tamagoyaki pan (or small nonstick skillet) over medium heat. Brush with a thin layer of oil using a paper towel or brush.
3. Pour in roughly 1/3 of the egg mixture. Tilt pan to spread evenly.
4. When the surface is just barely set (still wet on top), use chopsticks or a spatula to roll the omelette toward you, creating a log at the near edge.
5. Push the log to the far edge of the pan. Oil the now-empty pan surface.
6. Pour in another 1/3 of egg mixture, lifting the existing log so new egg flows underneath it. Let set slightly, then roll the log back toward you, incorporating the new layer.
7. Repeat with remaining egg mixture.
8. Transfer to a bamboo rolling mat. Roll tightly and shape into a rectangle. Let rest 2–3 minutes.
9. Slice into 2–3 cm thick rounds. Serve with grated daikon and soy sauce.
