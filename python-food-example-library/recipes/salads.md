# Salads and Bowls

## Caesar Salad
Cuisine: Italian-American | Technique: no-cook | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: Dressing: 2 cloves garlic, 4 anchovy fillets, 1 tsp Dijon mustard, 1 egg yolk, 2 tbsp lemon juice, 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce, 100ml neutral oil, 30ml EVOO, salt, pepper Salad: 2 heads Romaine lettuce (inner leaves only), 80g Parmesan (freshly grated), Croutons: 4 thick slices sourdough, 3 tbsp olive oil, 2 cloves garlic (minced), salt

Instructions:
1. Croutons: Tear bread into rough 2cm chunks. Toss with olive oil, garlic, and salt.
2. Croutons: Spread on a baking tray. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 12–15 minutes, tossing once, until golden and crunchy. Set aside.
3. Dressing: On a cutting board, mince garlic and anchovies together. Add a pinch of salt. Using the side of the knife, work them into a smooth paste.
4. Dressing: Transfer to a bowl. Whisk in egg yolk, lemon juice, Worcestershire, and mustard.
5. Dressing: Slowly drizzle in neutral oil while whisking constantly, then olive oil. Dressing should be creamy and emulsified.
6. Dressing: Whisk in 2 tbsp grated Parmesan. Season with salt, pepper, and more lemon if needed.
7. Assembly: Tear Romaine into large pieces or leave whole inner leaves.
8. Assembly: In a large bowl, toss lettuce with just enough dressing to coat — don't drown it.
9. Assembly: Add most of the Parmesan and croutons. Toss gently.
10. Assembly: Transfer to plates. Garnish with remaining Parmesan and extra croutons.

## Greek Salad (Horiatiki)
Cuisine: Greek | Technique: no-cook | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Very Easy | Diet: vegan, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 0

Ingredients: 4 large ripe tomatoes (cut in wedges), 1 cucumber (cut in thick rounds, halved), 1/2 red onion (thinly sliced), 100g Kalamata olives (pitted), 200g feta (in a block, not crumbled), 1 tsp dried oregano, 5 tbsp EVOO, 2 tbsp red wine vinegar, salt, black pepper, Optional: 1 green bell pepper (sliced), fresh mint

Instructions:
1. Combine tomatoes, cucumber, onion, and olives in a large bowl.
2. Season with salt, pepper, and oregano. Toss gently.
3. Arrange on a platter. Place the feta block on top whole (or in one large chunk) — do not crumble it. Authentic Greek salad has a slab of feta, not crumbles.
4. Drizzle with olive oil and vinegar.
5. Add another pinch of oregano over the feta. Serve with crusty bread. Note: No lettuce in a proper Horiatiki. Let ingredients sit 5 minutes before eating — the salt draws out juices and creates a natural dressing. PANZANELLA (Tuscan Bread Salad)

## Niçoise Salad
Cuisine: Provençal | Technique: assembling | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 200g oil-packed tuna, 4 eggs, 200g haricots verts (or green beans), 300g new potatoes, 100g Niçoise olives, 4 anchovy fillets, 2 medium tomatoes (quartered), 1/2 red onion (thinly sliced), 2 tbsp capers, 8 large Romaine or butter lettuce leaves Dressing: 1 tsp Dijon mustard, 2 tbsp red wine vinegar, 6 tbsp EVOO, salt, pepper

Instructions:
1. Cook potatoes in salted boiling water until just tender, about 15 minutes. Drain and halve while warm. Dress immediately with 1 tbsp vinaigrette.
2. Blanch haricots verts in boiling salted water 2–3 minutes until bright green but still crisp. Plunge into ice water. Drain.
3. Soft-boil eggs: bring water to boil, lower eggs gently, cook 7 minutes. Transfer to ice water. Peel and halve.
4. Make vinaigrette: whisk mustard and vinegar together. Drizzle in olive oil while whisking. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Arrange all components on a large platter: lettuce base, then potatoes, green beans, tomatoes, olives, tuna, capers, and halved eggs.
6. Lay anchovy fillets decoratively on top.
7. Drizzle vinaigrette over everything just before serving. Note: The salad should never be tossed — it's composed. Each element distinct. TABBOULEH (Lebanese)

## Panzanella
Cuisine: Tuscan | Technique: soaking bread | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min + 30 min rest | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients: 400g day-old rustic bread (Tuscan or sourdough), 600g ripe tomatoes (various sizes and colors), 1/2 cucumber (seeded, diced), 1/2 red onion (thinly sliced), 20 fresh basil leaves, 5 tbsp EVOO, 3 tbsp red wine vinegar, salt, black pepper, 6 anchovy fillets (optional)

Instructions:
1. Tear bread into rough 3cm chunks. If fresh, spread on a tray and dry in a 300°F oven for 10 minutes. It should be stale and dry, not toasted hard.
2. Place bread in a large bowl. Sprinkle with 2 tbsp each olive oil and vinegar and a good pinch of salt. Toss and let the bread soak for 10 minutes.
3. Halve cherry tomatoes; quarter larger ones. Salt them and let sit in a colander 5 minutes — they'll release juice, which becomes part of the dressing.
4. Add tomatoes and their juices to the bread. Add cucumber, red onion, and anchovies.
5. Dress with remaining olive oil and vinegar. Tear in basil.
6. Season generously with salt and pepper. Toss everything together.
7. Let rest 20–30 minutes before serving — the bread should absorb all the juices and become custardy-soft, not soggy. Serve at room temperature.

## Som Tum (Green Papaya Salad)
Cuisine: Thai | Technique: pounding in mortar | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Medium (requires mortar) | Serves: 2–4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 0

Ingredients: 1 small green papaya (about 400g, peeled and shredded into thin julienne), 10 cherry tomatoes (halved), 100g long beans or green beans (cut in 3cm pieces), 3 cloves garlic, 2–6 bird's eye chilis (adjust to tolerance), 2 tbsp dried shrimp, 2 tbsp fish sauce, 3 tbsp lime juice, 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar), 2 tbsp roasted peanuts (crushed), sticky rice for serving

Instructions:
1. Use a julienne peeler or a box grater to shred peeled green papaya into fine strips. Alternatively, score the papaya lengthwise with a sharp knife then shave across.
2. In a large mortar and pestle: pound garlic and chilis into a rough paste.
3. Add long beans. Bruise them gently with the pestle — you want them slightly cracked, not pulverized.
4. Add tomatoes. Pound gently until tomatoes just burst.
5. Add dried shrimp. Add sugar, fish sauce, and lime juice. Mix with spoon and pestle.
6. Add shredded papaya. Use the pestle to bruise and pound while turning with a spoon (toss-and-pound technique). Everything should be lightly bruised and dressed.
7. Taste: should be simultaneously sour, salty, sweet, and hot. Adjust aggressively.
8. Transfer to plates. Top with crushed peanuts. Serve with sticky rice.

## Korean Japchae
Cuisine: Korean | Technique: stir-frying + assembling | Time: 15 min

Ingredients: 1 cup glass noodles, spinach, mushrooms, carrot, 4 egg, 500g beef (), soy, 1 onion, 2 cloves garlic, 1 lemon, gochujang

Instructions:
1. Prep: slice proteins thin and cut vegetables evenly. Mix sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
2. Heat a wok/skillet over high heat. Add a thin film of oil.
3. Cook protein first until just browned. Remove to a plate.
4. Add aromatics (garlic/ginger/onion). Stir 15–30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add vegetables from firm to tender. Stir-fry until crisp-tender.
6. Return protein to the pan. Pour in sauce and toss until glossy and cooked through.
7. Finish with herbs/sesame/lime (if used). Serve hot with rice/noodles.

Ingredients: beets, chèvre, arugula, walnuts, balsamic, 1 onion, 2 cloves garlic, 1 lemon

Instructions:
1. Prep and wash all produce. Chop/slice core ingredients to bite-size pieces.
2. Make dressing: whisk oil + acid (lemon/vinegar) + salt + pepper (and any listed spices) until emulsified.
3. Toss vegetables/greens with dressing just before serving.
4. Add any proteins/cheese/croutons/nuts listed. Toss gently again.
5. Taste and adjust salt/acid. Serve immediately.
