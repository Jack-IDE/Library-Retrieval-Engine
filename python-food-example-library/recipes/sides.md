# Side Dishes and Snacks

## Creamed Spinach
Cuisine: American steakhouse | Technique: sautéing + cream reduction | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 5 min | Cook: 15 min

Ingredients: 1 kg fresh spinach (or 600g frozen, fully thawed), 200ml heavy cream, 1 shallot (minced), 2 cloves garlic (minced), 30g butter, 1/4 tsp nutmeg, 40g Parmesan (grated), salt, pepper

Instructions:
1. Blanch fresh spinach in boiling water 1 minute. Drain. Squeeze out ALL water with hands. Roughly chop. (If frozen: thaw completely, squeeze dry.)
2. Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat. Cook shallot until soft, 3 minutes. Add garlic, 1 min.
3. Add cream. Simmer until reduced by 1/3, about 5 minutes.
4. Add squeezed spinach. Stir to combine. Cook 3 minutes.
5. Add nutmeg and Parmesan. Season generously with salt and pepper.
6. Taste — it should be rich, savory, and very slightly sharp with nutmeg.

## Crispy Roast Potatoes
Cuisine: British | Technique: parboiling + high-heat roasting | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Serves: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 1 hr

Ingredients: 1 kg Maris Piper or russet potatoes (peeled, cut in large chunks), 4 tbsp duck fat or goose fat or vegetable oil, 1 tsp fine salt, 1 tbsp semolina or flour (optional — for extra crunch)

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Put the fat in a large roasting tray. Place in oven.
2. Put potato chunks in a large pot. Cover with cold salted water. Bring to boil.
3. Boil 8–10 minutes until surface begins to soften but interior is not cooked through.
4. Drain completely. Return to the warm empty pot. Shake the pot vigorously with the lid on — this roughens up the surface dramatically. The rougher the surface, the crispier the potato.
5. Sprinkle with semolina or flour if using. Toss to coat.
6. Remove the smoking-hot tray from the oven. Carefully add potatoes. The fat will spatter. Toss to coat in hot fat.
7. Roast 45–55 minutes, turning twice, until all sides are deep golden and incredibly crispy.
8. Remove with a slotted spoon. Season with flaky salt. Serve immediately. Note: These cannot be made in advance. Once they sit, they soften. POLENTA (Classic Northern Italian)

## Mashed Potato (Robuchon Style)
Cuisine: French (Joël Robuchon) | Technique: boiling + pressing + enriching | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Medium | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 4 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 25 min

Ingredients: 1 kg Ratte or Yukon Gold potatoes (peeled), 250g cold unsalted butter (cubed!), 200ml whole milk (warmed), fine salt, white pepper

Instructions:
1. Start potatoes in cold salted water. Bring to boil. Cook 25 minutes until very tender.
2. Drain. Pass through a fine-mesh food mill or ricer (NEVER a food processor — it makes gluey paste).
3. Return riced potato to the pot over very low heat. Stir dry for 2–3 minutes to evaporate any remaining moisture. The potato must be completely dry.
4. Begin adding cold butter cubes — one or two at a time. Beat vigorously with a wooden spoon after each addition. The potato will absorb an astonishing amount of butter.
5. Once all butter is incorporated, slowly pour in warm milk, beating constantly.
6. Season with salt and white pepper.
7. Pass through a fine sieve for the silkiest result. The ratio: Robuchon's is 1:1 potato to butter. This is not a mistake. This is the point. CREAMED SPINACH (Steakhouse Style)

## Potato Gratin Dauphinois
Cuisine: French (Dauphiné) | Technique: slow baking in cream | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free-ish | Serves: 6–8 | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 1.5 hrs

Ingredients: 1.5 kg Yukon Gold or waxy potatoes, 500ml heavy cream, 250ml whole milk, 3 garlic cloves (2 minced, 1 halved), 1/2 tsp freshly grated nutmeg, 100g Gruyère (grated — optional but delicious), 30g unsalted butter, salt, white pepper

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
2. Rub a gratin dish generously with the halved garlic clove, then with softened butter.
3. Peel potatoes and slice thinly — 3mm — on a mandoline if possible. Do NOT rinse slices. The starch is essential for the sauce to thicken.
4. Combine cream, milk, minced garlic, nutmeg, salt, and white pepper in a saucepan. Bring to a simmer. Remove from heat.
5. Layer potato slices in the dish, overlapping like roof tiles. Season each layer lightly.
6. Pour the warm cream mixture over — it should come just to the top layer.
7. Top with Gruyère if using.
8. Bake uncovered for 1.5 hours until the top is deep golden, the cream has thickened to a rich sauce, and a sharp knife slides through potatoes with zero resistance.
9. Rest 15 minutes before serving — it's molten inside. CRISPY ROAST POTATOES (British Method)
