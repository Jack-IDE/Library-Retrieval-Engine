# Bread, Pastry and Baked Goods

## Croissants
Cuisine: French/Austrian | Technique: laminating + baking | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Very Hard | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: makes 12 | Prep: 2 days | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: Détrempe: 500g strong bread flour, 10g fine salt, 75g sugar, 10g instant yeast, 200ml cold whole milk, 100ml cold water, 50g unsalted butter (softened) Butter block (beurrage): 280g European-style unsalted butter (84% fat) Egg wash: 2 egg yolks + 2 tbsp heavy cream

Instructions:
1. Day 1: Combine flour, salt, sugar, yeast. Add cold milk and water. Mix to shaggy dough. Add softened butter. Knead 5 minutes — just developed, not fully smooth. Flatten to a rectangle, wrap in plastic, refrigerate overnight.
2. Day 1: Butter block: Beat butter with rolling pin into a 20×20cm square. Refrigerate. Both dough and butter must be the same cold-but-pliable consistency.
3. Day 2 — Lamination: Roll dough into a 40×20cm rectangle. Place butter block in the center. Fold dough over butter like a letter (first fold = lock-in).
4. Day 2 — Lamination: Roll out to 60×20cm. Fold into thirds (book fold = turn 1). Wrap, refrigerate 30 min.
5. Day 2 — Lamination: Repeat: roll out to 60×20cm, fold into thirds (turn 2). Wrap, refrigerate 30 min.
6. Day 2 — Lamination: Repeat: roll out to 60×20cm, fold into thirds (turn 3). Wrap, refrigerate 1 hr minimum. You now have 3³ = 27 layers of butter.
7. Day 2 — Lamination: Roll dough out to 4mm thick. Cut into tall isoceles triangles (base ~12cm, height ~25cm).
8. Day 2 — Lamination: Make a small notch at the base of each triangle. Roll from base to tip — gently stretch as you roll. The tip should end up underneath.
9. Day 2 — Lamination: Curve the ends to form the classic crescent shape. Place on parchment-lined trays.
10. Day 2 — Lamination: Prove at room temperature 2–3 hours until visibly puffed and jiggle when shaken. (Test: should wobble when pan is moved — this means layers have separated.)
11. Day 2 — Lamination: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush gently with egg wash.
12. Day 2 — Lamination: Bake 18–22 minutes until deep amber. They should look almost too dark.

## Focaccia al Rosmarino
Cuisine: Ligurian | Technique: dimpling + olive oil bath + baking | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 8–10 | Prep: 15 min + 2 hrs rise | Cook: 25 min

Ingredients: 500g bread flour, 7g instant yeast, 400ml warm water, 8g salt, 80ml EVOO (divided), Fresh rosemary sprigs, flaky sea salt (Maldon), optional: sliced olives, cherry tomatoes

Instructions:
1. Combine flour, yeast, and salt. Mix in water and 3 tbsp olive oil until shaggy.
2. Knead by hand 8–10 minutes (or stand mixer 5 min) until smooth and elastic.
3. Pour 2 tbsp olive oil into a large bowl. Add dough, turning to coat in oil.
4. Cover and rest at room temperature 1–1.5 hours until doubled.
5. Generously oil an 30×40cm baking tray (use all remaining oil). Transfer dough.
6. Stretch dough across the tray with your fingers — it will resist. Let rest 10 minutes, then stretch again. It will fill the pan more easily now.
7. Cover and rest 30–45 more minutes until puffy.
8. Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C).
9. Press fingers all the way down into the dough at 3cm intervals creating deep dimples. Don't be shy — real dimples, not gentle pokes.
10. Drizzle with more olive oil (it will pool in the dimples — this is correct and essential).
11. Press rosemary sprigs into dimples. Scatter flaky salt generously.
12. Bake 20–25 minutes until deep golden and the edges are crispy.

## Naan
Cuisine: Indian | Technique: tandoor or cast iron skillet | Time: 60+ min | Difficulty: Easy | Diet: vegetarian | Serves: 4 (makes 8 naans) | Prep: 15 min + 1.5 hrs rise | Cook: 20 min

Ingredients: 400g all-purpose flour, 7g instant yeast, 1 tsp sugar, 200ml warm water, 100g plain yogurt, 1 egg, 2 tbsp melted butter or ghee, 1 tsp salt Garlic butter: 60g butter, 3 cloves garlic (minced), fresh cilantro

Instructions:
1. Combine flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Make a well.
2. Add water, yogurt, egg, and butter. Mix to form a soft, slightly sticky dough.
3. Knead 8–10 minutes until smooth and supple. The dough should be softer than bread dough.
4. Cover and rise at room temperature 1.5 hours until doubled.
5. Divide into 8 balls. Roll out each ball to an oval, about 5mm thick.
6. Heat a cast iron skillet over high heat until smoking.
7. Place one naan in the dry pan. Cook 1–2 minutes until large bubbles form and char spots develop.
8. Flip. Cook 1–2 more minutes. It should have beautiful blistered char marks.
9. Remove and brush immediately with garlic butter. Scatter cilantro.
10. Keep warm in a towel while making the rest. Serve immediately.
